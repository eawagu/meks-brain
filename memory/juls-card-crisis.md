## Pattern

The JULS Card Crisis is a recurring failure event affecting [[JULS]] settlement rails — specifically [[JULS ACCESS]] and [[JULS FCMB]] — while non-JULS providers ([[ISW]], [[HABARIPAY]]) remain stable. The clean separation between JULS and non-JULS providers points to a shared infrastructure failure within the JULS network.

## Observed instances

| Date | Event |
|---|---|
| Wk1 (Apr 2026) | JULS ACCESS peak failure 63.64%. |
| Apr 19, 2026 | JULS FCMB spike to 14.29%. |
| Apr 23, 2026 | **Worst recorded:** JULS ACCESS 68.18% (exceeded Wk1 peak). JULS FCMB 25% same day. Two providers collapsing in concert is a strong indicator of shared infrastructure failure. JULS FCMB weekly average jumped to 14.25% (vs 1.54% prior week). |

## Insulated providers (week of Apr 24)

- [[ISW]] — held steady at 1.88% average, no spikes
- [[HABARIPAY]] — 0.72% (slight improvement)

## Implication

Fault line is within the JULS network, not industry-wide. Investigation should focus on shared infrastructure between JULS providers.
