## Overview

Platformization is the organizing principle behind [[Project Phoenix]]: one configurable product, built by a global platform org, consumed by in-country business entities as tenants. It replaces separate per-country builds (e.g., separate Nigeria and UK) with a shared product + country-specific configuration.

## Structural Implications

- **Nigeria becomes a business entity** within the MFB — not a product-engineering org.
- **CBO role** prepared for [[Toby]] to lead the Nigeria business entity.
- **4–5 business units** per entity: business banking, personal banking, digital banking, market research, operations, etc.
- **Platform org** centralizes product/engineering; business lines own strategy + customer response.
- **[[MoneyWalls]]** migrates to the platform model — business team operates MoneyWalls on a separate platform team's infrastructure.
- **Code freeze** of 2–3 months expected during platformization; BAU "keep the lights on" work preserved.

## Framing

- Described by [[Tosin Eniolorunda]] as "open-heart surgery" and "changing turbine engines at 45,000 feet."
- Enables global scale by 2026 — per-country cost falls as platformization lowers incremental-market cost.
