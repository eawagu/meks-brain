## Definition

M1–M6 retention measures month-over-month cohort retention — what percentage of users acquired in a given month are still active at month 1, month 2, through month 6. M0 is the acquisition month (100% baseline).

## Benchmarks by Category

| | M1 | M3 | M6 |
|--|--|--|--|
| B2B SaaS | 70–85% | 60–75% | 55–70% |
| FinTech (payments, lending, wallets) | 50–70% | 35–55% | 30–50% |
| Consumer apps (social, entertainment) | 25–40% | 10–20% | 5–15% |
| E-commerce / marketplaces | 30–40% | 20–30% | 15–25% |

Enterprise SaaS retains better than SMB-focused. Consumer retention is brutal — most churn happens in the first week.

## Curve Shape Diagnostics

- **Steep early drop then flattening** — retained core exists. Focus on onboarding to pull more users into that core. Healthy pattern.
- **Steady decline that never flattens** — no natural retention floor. Product lacks a habit loop or switching cost. Red flag.
- **Drop-off at a specific month** — structural cause (contract end, seasonal use, UX failure). Diagnosable and fixable.

The flattening (plateau) is the key signal — it means product-market fit for a segment. If M3→M6 is still declining steeply, the product hasn't earned habitual use yet.

## FinTech Application

M1–M6 is often the primary health metric for lending, payments, and wallet products. A user who transacts in M3 is worth multiples of one who churns in M1 because CAC is already sunk. The strategic question: what's the minimum activation event that predicts M6 retention? That's where product intervention focus belongs.
