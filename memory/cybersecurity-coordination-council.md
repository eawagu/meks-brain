## Summary

A press release from the Federal Ministry of Communications, Innovation & Digital Economy announcing the Federal Government's intention to establish a non-statutory Cybersecurity Coordination Council. The Council aims to strengthen Nigeria's collective cyber resilience through multi-stakeholder coordination, trusted threat intelligence sharing, and capacity building, with a national roundtable planned for April 2026.

## Key Points

- The Council is envisioned as a non-statutory, multi-stakeholder coordination platform — not a regulatory body
- Initiative follows recent cybersecurity incidents that disrupted major private institutions and public systems in Nigeria
- Proposed participants include CISOs, cybersecurity professional associations, Nigerian Computer Society, international OEMs, digital security researchers, law enforcement, civil society, and government agencies
- Key deliverables: trusted threat intelligence sharing mechanisms, sector-wide cyber defence protocols, capacity-building programmes, operational coordination for incident response, and governance alignment frameworks
- [[NITDA]], [[NCC]], [[Galaxy Backbone]], and [[NDPC]] directed to jointly establish a technical coordination secretariat
- Secretariat domiciled within NITDA, under strategic coordination of the Minister's Office
- National cybersecurity industry roundtable planned for April 2026 as formal commencement of stakeholder consultations
- Minister Dr Bosun Tijani: "Cybersecurity is a shared national responsibility"
