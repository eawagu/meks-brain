## Definition

Third Party Processing (TPP) operates at two levels at [[TeamApt]]:

1. **Department** (business line): One of four core departments, led by [[Tracy Ojaigho]]. An Enabler department (alongside Domestic Switching). Owns acquirer processing, issuer processing, ACT, and Non-Bank Acquiring.
2. **Operations function**: Manages payment transactions processed through external switching networks — [[Interswitch]], Habaripay, CoralPay, and IDRS/DCIR. Handles settlement reconciliation, chargeback management, and incident reporting.

## Department Scope (Business Line)

Products: Acquirer processing, Issuer processing (CMS, EMV Data Prep), ACT, MPGS dashboard, Non-Bank Acquiring. Key systems: Aptent, ACT, EMV Data Prep, Rsync CMS. Customers: Acquiring/issuing banks, card schemes, personalization bureaus.

CBN licenses supporting TPP: Switching & Processing (foundation), Non-Bank Acquiring.

## Core Principles (from Doctrine)

Seven operating principles anchored on: accuracy over speed, settlement integrity (traceable, reconciled, supported, explainable), auditability of every financial movement, and regulatory compliance as a floor.
