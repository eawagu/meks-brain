## Definition

A career ladder is the ordered sequence of levels an employee can advance through, with each rung representing a meaningful increase in scope, autonomy, or leadership. A well-designed ladder makes advancement criteria explicit and observable rather than tenure-based.

## Key Design Choices

- **Single ladder vs. dual track**: some organizations have one ladder; others split into IC and management tracks at senior levels (e.g., [[Moniepoint]]'s 051 Principal / 050 Manager split)
- **Level granularity**: too many levels creates ambiguity at boundaries; too few creates long stagnation periods
- **Level equivalence**: when IC and management tracks run in parallel, defining explicit equivalence (same compensation band, same organizational weight) signals that both paths are legitimate
