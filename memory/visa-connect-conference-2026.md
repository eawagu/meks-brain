## Summary

Full conference notes from [[Visa]] Connect Conference 2026, themed "The Future of Digital Ecosystems." 13 sessions covering regional overview (30 markets, 600M people, $1T GDP), reimagined payments (VPay, stablecoins, agentic commerce), tokenisation scaling, fraud fighting (Visa Protect, FeatureSpace acquisition), data/AI/cybersecurity, B2B commercial solutions ($570B Africa opportunity), and brand/marketing strategy for Gen Z.

## Key Points

- West & Central Africa: cash still ~70% of PCE (down from 90% pre-COVID); goal <50% by 2030
- Cards = $37M payments; wallets 3x cards; cash 3x wallets
- [[Opay]] has more cards than any entity in Nigeria; fintechs capture 22% of personal consumption expenditure
- **Stablecoins**: $250B transacted; Visa linking cards to stablecoin credentials; instant settlement via USDC live in CEMEA, open for Nigeria — "jointly approach central bank"
- **Agentic Commerce**: 5 new Visa APIs for AI agent payments (authentication, tokenisation, personalisation, payment instructions, payment signals); pilots in Africa and North America
- **VPay**: virtual card issuance in <2 seconds, standalone or embeddable
- **Tokenisation**: foundation for mobile payments, instant issuance, agentic commerce; Absa Bank Ghana case study
- **FeatureSpace** (Visa acquisition): adaptive behavioural analytics, self-training fraud models, 360° customer view
- Visa invested $10B in network security over 5 years; detected 90M+ attacks in one month
- **B2B**: $570B opportunity across Visa's African markets; 96% are micro/small businesses
- [[Monnify]] case study presented: travel = 40-50% of volume, virtual cards for reconciliation, expanding Nigeria → Kenya
- [[Stanbic Bank]] (IBTC): large corporate program, 14% YoY payment volume increase
- Gen Z = 1/3 of population, 20% spending, 42% of digital wallet usage
- Government as key vertical: DRC healthcare case study (health insurance card as credential with biometrics)
- Visa Cashback Card-Linked Offers launching June 2026 — no bank technical integration required
- Visa positioning as full-stack technology partner, not just card network
- TeamApt not on partner logo graphic — only MoniePoint shown
