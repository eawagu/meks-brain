## Summary

Comprehensive 2025 business context for [[TeamApt]]. Organized into 5 product units: MADD and [[Monnify]] (revenue drivers), AptPay Suite, TPP, and Juliana (enablers). Revenue target ₦24.2B–₦30B; actual estimated ₦10–12B (~35–50% attainment). Includes detailed revenue models, year-end results per business, bank/partner relationships, and 10 cross-cutting lessons.

## Key Points

- 2025 revenue targets: Monnify ₦11B, DD ₦6B, Direct to Bank ₦3.7B, Switch ₦2.5B, TPP ₦1B = ₦24.2B (legal entity target ₦30B)
- Starting position: ₦422M annual revenue, -134% EBITDA
- **Monnify:** ₦9.05B achieved vs ₦13B target (79%); VTU merchant exits (MTN/Airtel) drove decline; card success rate improved 35%→84.42%; payout success 99.99%
- **Direct Debit:** Revenue exceeded 200% of KR driven by Access Bank; auth success 95.19%; 20 billers onboarded but 0% actively transacting
- **TPP:** Visa + Mastercard acquirer processor certifications completed; MPGS live; EMV Data Prep all modules done except Visa perso; acquiring licenses: Visa ✅, MC pending
- **Domestic Switching:** 37% of Moniepoint POS via Juliana (target 70%); 16 domestic issuers; TACHA beta released; Account Switch 99.89% success; only 2/10 NSS banks operational
- DD revenue model: ₦17.50/transaction (₦25 merchant fee - ₦7.50 issuer fee), 10% MoM growth target
- Key lesson: Integration ≠ Activation (20 billers onboarded, 0% transacting)
- Monnify concentration risk: >50% volume in VTU segment caused outsized impact when disrupted
- DevRel massively overperformed: CSAT 94% vs 50% target, NPS 88% vs 20% target
- By 2026, restructured from 5 units to 4 departments: DD/CDD, Monnify, TPP, Domestic Switching
- CBN IT Standards maturity level 4 (benchmark: 2 for PSSPs); Nigeria removed from FATF grey list Oct 2025
