## Summary

Frank Atashili's strategic note to self consolidating what Ravi Jakhodia expects from him following the April 7 TSP planning meetings. Captures explicit deliverables, implicit signals, a 7-day preparation checklist, and explicit "don'ts." The core framing: Ravi pushes for clarity, realism, and process discipline — he wants Frank to own planning and stakeholder management while Alex owns engineering execution.

## Key Points

- **Core message from Ravi:** Without structured plan and formal validation, TSP risks being incomplete, misaligned with leadership on timeline, or built without business-leader endorsement that protects the team later.
- **Explicit Deliverable 1 — Detailed Project Plan (HIGHEST PRIORITY):** Lists all integrations, in-scope/out-of-scope, justifies why selected scope proves TSP fit-for-purpose. Draft by Apr 7–8, review Friday Apr 11. Status: first draft created ([[TSP Phase 1 Project Plan DRAFT (Apr 10)]]). Missing: Alex's scoping perspective, Ravi V's capability mapping, specific integration list, finalized engineer names.
- **Explicit Deliverable 2 — Formal Business Leader Validation:** Non-negotiable for Ravi. Package reverse-engineered capabilities; present to BLs for formal sign-off. Sessions weeks 2–3 (Apr 14–25). Ravi's exact position: "If we're building a TSP and the current business leaders don't even have the time to give it, then how serious are we as an organization?" BLs to engage: Khalil (VAS, engaged), Solomon+1 (Account Switching, needs securing), Femi Davies/Damilola Oyediran (Card infra), Damilare Ogunnaike (Atlas/Iris Monnify org).
- **Explicit Deliverable 3 — Team Brief:** Meet the assembled product engineering team. This week (after Apr 7). Kickoff for the 2 dev teams.
- **Explicit Deliverable 4 — Company-Wide Briefing:** Brief entire company on team structure and plan. Originally Thursday Apr 10 — needs to accommodate Frank's travel (Canada Apr 8, London Sunday Apr 13).
- **Explicit Deliverable 5 — Schedule Meetings:** Internal team review Friday Apr 11. Leadership meeting (Tosin + Felix) Monday Apr 14 afternoon. Ravi J assists with availability.
- **Explicit Deliverable 6 — Honest Timeline Communication:** Not conservative, not unrealistic. Original deadline end-May. Alex's estimate 3 months. Resources smaller than planned. 1 week elapsed. Frank's Monday pitch: accept 3mo timeline (~early July), gap from 2mo = 4 weeks, options are accept/add resources/reduce scope. Recommendation: accept 3mo — reusable engine architecture delivers significantly more capability.
- **Implicit — Ravi wants Frank to lead, not just participate:** "Frank, if you can take the lead on this in terms of helping us plan all of this." Frank bridges engineering (Alex) with business/stakeholder alignment.
- **Implicit — Ravi's "safer route":** Frank + Alex private read — Ravi pushed for OKRs and formal sign-offs but stopped short of fighting for 80% engineer time. Advocacy through structure, not direct confrontation with Felix/Tosin on resources. Implication: don't expect Ravi to solve resource problem; he sets up governance/escalation frameworks but resource negotiation remains Frank+Alex's fight.
- **Implicit — Phase 1 must unblock Stage 2:** Ravi's deepest concern: "If you're not clear about what is the outcome of TSP by the end of Stage 1, then my worry is that when say Payments picks it up in Stage 2, how will they really deliver." API contracts, adapter specs, integration patterns must be documented before Phase 1 ends.
- **What Frank should NOT do:** (1) Don't promise 2mo delivery — Ravi respects honesty over optimism. (2) Don't skip BL validation. (3) Don't wait for resources to materialize. (4) Don't let the London strategy conference (Apr 14–16) consume momentum — internal review Friday, leadership Monday before conference takes over.
