## Definition

Performance Management is the organizational system for evaluating, developing, and calibrating employee performance — encompassing goal-setting, periodic reviews, feedback mechanisms, and remediation paths. An industry-standard HR discipline implemented across organizations with varying tools and cadences.

## Core Components (Generic)

- **Goal-setting** — typically via OKRs, MBOs, or similar frameworks
- **Periodic reviews** — quarterly, semi-annual, or annual cycles
- **Feedback mechanisms** — peer, upward, downward, manager effectiveness surveys
- **Remediation paths** — Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs), coaching, role changes, termination
- **Calibration** — cross-team normalization to prevent rating inflation or disparities

## Interaction with Other Concepts

Performance management intersects with resource allocation (underperformers affect capacity planning), hiring pipelines (backfill decisions), and organizational design (role scope changes after PIPs).
