## Summary

Comparative analysis: [[Transaction Switching Platform]] as orchestration layer (Layer 2) vs TeamApt Transaction Switch as switching layer (Layer 1). Complementary architecture, not competition. TeamApt owns wire protocols, authorization, scheme connectivity; TSP owns fund movement orchestration.

## Key Points

- 3-layer architecture: Product Experience / Fund Movement / Switching
- TSP = Layer 2 (orchestration), TeamApt Switch = Layer 1 (switching)
- Blind spots identified: dual account switches (Juliana vs Atlas), MFB parallel card infrastructure (Postilion, CMS Manager, Aptent, Iris)
- CBN license scope implications
- Wire protocol vs fund movement separation principle
- Budget rebuild risk flagged
- Dual-generation problem documented
