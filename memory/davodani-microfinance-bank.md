Nigerian microfinance bank (DDMFB) undergoing digital transformation in 2026 — website redesign, AI-driven CX, core banking platform sourcing, cybersecurity strengthening.

## 2026 IT Strategy — CTO Assessment (Feb 2026)
Per [[DDMFB_2026_IT_Strategy_Review]], the bank's 2026 IT Strategy document was reviewed and characterized as 'a wish list with price tags' lacking prioritization, timelines, KPIs, and risk assessment. Total documented spend ≈ ₦138.5M, with ~72% (₦100M) allocated to [[Core Banking System]] selection.

Section-by-section grades:
- SEO Strategy ₦5.5M — C+ (move to marketing)
- Website Restructure ₦7M — B- (phase with core banking)
- Customer Experience ₦5M — C (likely underestimated; needs ₦8–12M)
- Social Media & Marketing ₦8M — C+ (no attribution framework)
- Cybersecurity ₦13M — D+ (certs over tools — invert allocation)
- ATM Card Deployment TBD — D (no business case)
- Core Banking ₦100M — C- (deserves standalone workstream; vendor list erroneously includes ERP [[Microsoft Dynamics NAV]])

Critical gaps flagged: [[Disaster Recovery & Business Continuity]], [[NDPC Compliance]], IT staffing plan, integration architecture, [[CBN]] regulatory alignment, contingency budget.
