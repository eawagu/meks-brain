## Summary

Retreat Day 1 strategic session covering profitability targets, Platformization Phase 2 timeline, Kenya launch enablement, VAS/payments priorities, and competitor foreclosure tactics. [[Dennis Ajalie]] tasked with leading Verve pricing negotiation. Sumac Microfinance Bank acquisition confirms Kenya 2026 launch. Org split — global platform team vs country business entities — post-platformization.

## Key Points

- **PAT targets:** 20% before end of 2026 (raw rate), 30% "by Q7"/next year aspirational pending plan. @Money to develop the 30% plan via higher-margin lending.
- **Project Phoenix / Platformization phases:** Phase 1 underway — TSP launch for consolidated payments (account transfers, cards, other services), core platform + Phoenix design system across POS and web. **Phase 2 begins June** — company-wide adoption of new design system + AI-native processes. Phase 3 — beta new app + parallel backend to "strangle" legacy, progressive traffic cutover. Ravi appointed program manager.
- **Kenya launch:** Confirmed 2026 via [[Sumac Microfinance Bank]] acquisition. Peter + Ross acknowledged.
- **Competitive situation:** Competitor "Money Point" (distinct from Moneypoint) deploying foreclosure strategy via aggressive pricing — aim to deprive capital, gain merchant-side share. Weak anti-competition focus at CBN/FCCPC limits enforcement. Company engaging regulators on capital adequacy, foreclosure, monopoly risks. Regulator progress = potential sanctions.
- **GT/Jitsi dominance watch:** Despite zero-percent offers, share rose only ~0.3–0.4%. Monitor; apply competition law constraints if approaching ~10%.
- **Verve pricing:** Verve increased pricing tiers exploiting regulated-acquirer-unregulated-scheme loophole. [[Dennis Ajalie]] to lead negotiation contingent on volume. Company will not pass cost to customers given transfer/cash/cheaper competitor alternatives.
- **2026 operational priorities:** Retire MP35 POS + introduce new devices; real-time customer insight; reliability; faster launches; MoneyBook scaling; lending growth (target 1,000 then 2,000 loans per period vs 3,000 net new POS).
- **MoneyBook retention targets:** M1 80%, M6 60% before scaling acquisition. Current M1 ~67% ("poor"). Monthly validation and report required.
- **Key account management:** 10% of customers drive 55% of revenue — launching KAM program.
- **VAS:** Last-month growth 27% but underperforming relative to potential; UX + rewards initiative.
- **Nigeria revenue:** Trajectory toward $680M this year; ambition #1 in five years (from currently #3 by market share).
- **Org restructuring:** Nigeria as business entity under CBO; Toby prepared for CBO role. Business Banking, Personal Banking, Digital Banking, Market Research, Operations under CBO. Product/engineering + sales centralized. Strategy local to business lines.
- **Future market entries:** Acquisitions for licenses (Sumac model), not mergers. No mid-term large acquisitions. DRC cited as underserved post-platformization candidate.
- **Cost disciplines:** OPEX reductions, depreciation management, salary + cloud spend control (cloud up 10% last month). Contribution-margin improvement = largest lever. Deposits growing = margin driver (25% deposit increase in treasury yields pure margins).
- **Action: Pay with Moneypoint:** Account Transfers team to release.
- **Action: POS Team:** Deliver fixes + improve device experience.
- **Action: VAS Team:** Improve + finish launching international.
