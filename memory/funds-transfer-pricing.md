## Overview

Funds Transfer Pricing (FTP) is an internal "audit rate" used to compensate deposit-holders for cost-efficient funding and charge asset-holders for the capital they use. It is the analytical foundation of Moniepoint's new granular financial reporting.

## Mechanic

- Deposit-gathering houses receive FTP benefit (compensation for cheap funding)
- Asset-creating houses incur FTP charge (cost of funds)
- View toggle in new reporting: with FTP vs without FTP

## Moniepoint Rates

- FTP benefit to deposit-gathering houses: 30% of MPR
- FTP charge to asset-creating houses: MPR − 6

## Behavioral Use

- Lower FTP in a lending unit → incentivizes lower-rate lending (expand loan book)
- Higher FTP in a lending unit → forces selectivity on loan profitability (contract loan book to high-quality names)

## Practical View (Adrian/Savings Example)

- Without FTP: loss of 2.9 (interest expense + salaries)
- With FTP: 3.7 benefit — low-cost funds gathering recognized as positive contribution

## Allocation Examples

- POS depreciation (98% of fixed assets) → allocated to Card payments and POS transfer line within Account Payments only
- Deposit sources granularized: card business, account training business, Adriana, Money Bonds
