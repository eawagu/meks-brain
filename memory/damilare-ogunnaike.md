## Overview

Damilare Ogunnaike is currently the department lead for [[Monnify]] at [[TeamApt]] (one of the two Revenue Driver departments) and converts under [[Project Phoenix]] to **Head of [[Payment Gateway Platform]]** at the central platform org ([[MP Technologies UK]] / IPCo). The Monnify commercial layer stays with TeamApt OpCo under [[Daniel Ojinaka]]; the Monnify system becomes Payment Gateway Platform v0 — the starting substrate for the new group platform, which will be built out and eventually subsume Monnify's payment rails.

Co-presented the TeamApt CEO strategy deck at the [[2026 Executive Strategy Retreat]] alongside [[Dennis Ajalie]] (CEO) and [[Frank Atashili]] (CPO).

## Pre-Phoenix Department Scope (Monnify)

Products: Collections (VA, web card, DD), Disbursements, VAS, International (MPGS, Cybersource). Key systems: Monnify Gateway, Loom, Limit Engine, MPGS. Customers: Merchants, Payment Facilitators, VA provider banks.

## Strategy Retreat Presentation (Apr 15, 2026)

Presented the Monnify review and outlook section of the CEO deck. Key positions:

- **VA commoditization acknowledged:** Every competitor offers VAs at aggressive prices; some merchants issuing their own. Monnify's product breadth is constrained (missing international payments, overdraft, FX conversion, IoD).
- **Pivot strategy:** International payments, product lifecycle optimization, developer-focused marketing, performance fixes (VA rejected payments, payout reliability).
- **Q1 2026 performance:** Revenue ₦1.77B (130% of budget), merchants 4,650 (130%), but margin compression (gross 91%→81%, contribution 80%→65%).
- **Deposit mobilization:** ₦52.3B daily average deposit (Mar 2026). Top merchants: Kora Payments ₦7.04B, NALA ₦3.59B, Fincra ₦2.86B.
- **International payments milestones:** Visa 3DS completed, MPGS live, $20K+ first transactions. Targets: 5 merchants, $615K revenue, $38.9M value, 135K volume.
- **Business needs:** Product Growth Partner (merchant lifecycle), Product Managers, Engineering Lead.
