## Definition

A retention pattern discovered through Moniepoint customer data analysis: customers who engage with a Value-Added Service (airtime purchase, data top-up, bill payment) within their first 30 days retain at dramatically higher rates than "transfer only" customers. Presented at the [[2026 Executive Strategy Retreat]] (Apr 15, 2026).

## Evidence

- VAS customers: ~80% retention at M12
- Transfer-only customers: retention "collapses entirely" over the same period
- Hypothesis: the app where a user handles their VAS payments becomes their main financial account, driving broader engagement across all product lines

## Strategic Implications

[[Tosin Eniolorunda]] and the product team endorsed pivoting from treating VAS as a stapled-on feature to making it a core value proposition. Initiatives:

- Next ad campaign VAS-focused
- Expand biller catalog: subscriptions, e-commerce platforms, insurance, pensions
- Improve UX for discovery and navigation of VAS features
- Revamp USSD interface for VAS
- Integrate VAS into "honeymoon" early rewards for new users
- Center new P2P referral program around VAS (e.g., buy data to earn money)
- Personal users engage with VAS more intensely than business banking users — key differentiator for personal banking product

## Connection to [[M1-M6 Retention]]

This finding maps directly to the M1-M6 retention framework: VAS usage in M0 (first 30 days) is the strongest known predictor of M12 retention. The 80% figure places VAS-active Moniepoint users at the top of the FinTech benchmark range (30-50% typical at M6).
