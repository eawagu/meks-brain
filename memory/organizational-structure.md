## Definition

Organizational structure is the deliberate arrangement of roles, reporting relationships, and decision authorities within a company. It determines who coordinates with whom, who has authority over what, and how strategy gets translated into execution.

## Key Dimensions

- **Hierarchy depth**: flat (few levels between IC and CEO) vs. deep (many management layers)
- **Span of control**: how many direct reports each manager oversees
- **Centralization**: whether decisions are made at the top or delegated
- **Functional vs. divisional**: organized by function (engineering, marketing) or by business unit/product
