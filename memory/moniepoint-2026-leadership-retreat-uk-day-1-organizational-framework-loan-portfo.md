## Summary

Day 1 of [[Moniepoint 2026 Leadership Retreat UK]] established the organizational framework and eight judgment traits as the cultural and management operating system, and drove a deep review of the lending business. [[Tosin Eniolorunda]] walked leaders through the goal → structure → people → incentives → context → systems → governance model, then used case studies (UX/CSAT, card downtime, core banking outage, customer complaints) to show how to diagnose failures through the framework. The second half covered loan portfolio strategy: shift toward mid-sized customers, new [[Probability of Default Model]] and [[Affordability Model]] from [[Yuki]], asset-financing opportunity, big-ticket lending risk concerns.

## Key Points

- **Organizational Framework**: Goals, Structure, People, Incentives, Context, Systems, Governance — the seven management principles every leader must internalize.
- **Eight Judgment Traits for People**: Customer Obsession, Craft, Systemization, Urgency, Ownership, No Ego, Candor, Integrity.
- **Culture is defined by behavior, not wall plaques** — it is the emergent output of incentives and what is allowed or tolerated.
- **Structure serves goals, not individuals** — proposals to shape structure around specific "superstars" will be rejected.
- **Meritocracy mandate**: promotion based on performance, not on likability to manager.
- **Leaders mandated to define + cascade team goals immediately**, without waiting for [[Project Moose]] or other infrastructure.
- **Public praise, private admonishment** — Tosin's stated philosophy.
- **Talent density over tolerance**: if you're actively performance-managing someone, they should be out; no prolonged PIP.
- **Loan business status (end of March)**: ~1,000 active loans, YTD 63 billion (5% behind 66.3 billion target due to January "disaster month"). Target 180,000 active loans by year-end.
- **Sales productivity**: 1.8 loans/productive agent in March, goal 2–2.5 by year-end.
- **Ticket-size crash**: simplified volume-based sales incentive drove portfolio toward smallest/easiest loans, collapsing average ticket size. Mid-segment underserved and shrinking.
- **Mid-sized segment strategy**: price-drop campaign in progress to test elasticity; this segment holds the biggest value-growth lever.
- **NPLs rising**: acceptable for volume business (0.3% → 0.6% as volume doubled, willing up to 1–2%). Big-ticket NPLs problematic given 9% margin over treasury (29% yield – 20% cost of funds).
- **New PD Model (Yuki) launching next month** — demonstrated predictive power in backward tests. Debate: auto-decline highest-risk bucket vs. price for risk.
- **New Affordability Model (Yuki)** replacing simplistic turnover+time model; more variables → better loan sizing.
- **Digital lending product priority**: ~₦3,500 physical verification cost prohibitive at small tickets; digital product needed for reach.
- **Verification experiment**: one guarantor vs. two on loans up to 5M shows same cohort performance.
- **Asset financing opportunity**: solar + smartphones for SMEs; Shell Foundation credit guarantee. Partner capacity is the bottleneck — need ~300–2,000 units/day.
- **Big-ticket lending re-evaluation**: shift toward financing specific activities (e.g., PMS purchase from specific supplier) vs. cash disbursement; pay suppliers directly to tie funds to purpose.
- **Single obligor limit discipline**: any violation requires Board Credit Committee process — not ad-hoc.
- **Field credit officer bottleneck**: officers managing 400–500 loans (was 100); hiring fails in remote areas; exploring "Paavo model" (hire + train locals without prior experience).
- **Escalation proposal**: customer complaints should auto-escalate with transparency to customer, up to board level.
- **Design/UX case study**: CSAT vacant ~6 months, UX structured as agency not embedded in product, no org-wide qualitative-feedback system — full-framework diagnosis drives full-framework fix.
- **Card downtime case study**: engineer-inserted faulty DB records + SRE misdiagnosis = people + system problem; system must reject manual updates automatically.
- **Core banking outage case study**: context lived in individual memory — fix is accessible documented context, not better process.
