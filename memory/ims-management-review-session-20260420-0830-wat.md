## Summary

IMS Management Review Session on 2026-04-20 08:30 WAT — follow-up management-review walkthrough between [[Adefemi Opeogun]] (presenting the OPEX mock audit results), [[Emeka Awagu]] (CTO, management representative), [[Sarah Kojeku]] ([[OPEX]] team), [[Lateefat Adedeji-Oyedeji]], and [[Esther Ajibode]]. Mock audit on five ISO standards using PDCA approach. Results: 83.8% compliant, ~16% in progress, 1 minor non-conformity. Emeka's main objection: SWOT weakness framing (inadequate manpower, excessive automation diminishing human oversight, remote-work time-zone difficulties, full reliance on technology) needs reframing before external auditors see it.

- **Process stage**: gap analysis → awareness training → scope implementation → assessment → internal audit → management review (this session) → review of certification assessment → continuous improvement
- **Compliance breakdown**: 83.8% compliant (green); ~16% in progress (yellow); 1 minor non-conformity (red, already worked on)
- **SWOT weaknesses listed**: inadequate manpower vs rising demand; "excessive automation diminishes human interactions and oversight in quality management"; remote-work time-zone harmonization difficulty; full reliance on technology
- **Information security control measurement**: 1% non-existent, 1% initial, 8% defined, 73% managed, 17% optimized (~90% in green for managed+optimized)
- **Business continuity risk**: 1 minor + 4 moderate; SMS: 2 minor, 2 moderate, 1 major

- **Business impact analysis**: 14 services — 5 major, 7 critical, 2 moderate
- **Internal audit summary**: 0 major NC, 1 minor NC, 48 observations, 8 OFIs, 293 confirmed compliant, 1 N/A
- **Customer satisfaction**: 5% reported satisfaction rate; team addressing feedback via email; need to look at improving toward 85% target
- **Service uptime reporting**: delivered every Friday during all-hands; management present to query performance

## Emeka's Pushback

- Concerned about SWOT weakness framing — particularly "excessive automation": "we just need to define what that is" (no measurable definition)
- Disagrees that remote work and tech reliance are weaknesses — argued some should be strengths
- Concern: report is internal but auditors will see screen; wants to not give them anything "to pick up on unnecessarily"
- Request: reframe weaknesses before external [[Surveillance Audit]]
- "I don't know whether it's a weakness or it is a risk that we should be aware of"
