## Definition

The CEO KPI Scorecard is [[TeamApt]]'s Board-approved performance rubric for the MD/CEO. It defines measurable targets across 7 weighted categories covering 100% of CEO accountability and uses a 6-point rating scale (1.0 very materially below expectations → 6.0 extraordinary and exceptionally above). The Board uses it to evaluate annual CEO performance and calibrate compensation.

## Category Weights (FY2026)

| Category | Weight |
|---|---|
| Financial Performance & Profitability | 25% |
| Strategic Growth & Market Position | 20% |
| Technology Excellence & Operational Performance | 15% |
| Enterprise Client Growth & Retention | 15% |
| Corporate Governance, Risk & Compliance | 10% |
| Stakeholder Management & Brand | 8% |
| People, Culture & Organisational Health | 7% |

See [[note_2026-04-15T11-23-46-622Z]] for the full rubric with per-KPI sub-weights and scale definitions.

## Technology Section (15%) — CTO Interface

Three sub-KPIs, distributed across owners:
- **Platform Uptime & Reliability (6%)** — owned by [[Emeka Awagu]] (CTO)
- **Operational Efficiency & SLA Compliance (5%)** — owned by CTO
- **Technology Security & Cybersecurity Posture (4%)** — not CTO-owned

[[Project Phoenix]] and platform modernisation flow to a separate Platform Organisation (not the CTO pillar). [[Product Innovation Pipeline]] (6%) sits in the Strategic Growth section and flows to the [[Chief Product Officer]].

The result: CTO's direct footprint on the CEO scorecard is ~11% (6% + 5%), structured as a keep-the-lights-on mandate with no build/evolve component.

## Proposed Revisions (Apr 15, 2026)

Mek's review (see [[note_2026-04-15T11-42-42-517Z]]) proposes two rubric changes:

1. **Platform Uptime & Reliability** — Raise "Meets Expectations" threshold from 99.9% to 99.95%. Rationale: 99.9% (~8.75 hours downtime/year) is too soft for switching infrastructure banks depend on ([[Monnify]] at 99.99%, [[Atlas]] ~500M txn/month, [[Juliana Switch]] serving issuing banks).

2. **Operational Efficiency & SLA Compliance** — Add per-category floors at every rating level so a healthy composite cannot mask a failing domain (e.g., txn processing 99% + onboarding 75% averaging to "pass"). Specific floors: 3.0 ≥80% per category; 4.0 ≥85%; 5.0 ≥92%; 6.0 ≥97%.

Cybersecurity Posture rubric (4%) accepted as-is. Weight at 4% defensible for non-fund-holding infrastructure company, though 5% would better reflect scheme compliance exposure ([[Mastercard]], [[Verve]], [[Afrigo]]).

## Structural Observations

- 15% total technology weight in a technology infrastructure company is low but acceptable if the Board views technology as an enabler, not the product.
- The CEO scorecard structurally treats technology as a utility at "meets expectations" level — build/evolve sits elsewhere (CPO, Platform Org).
- Ratings translate to narrative descriptors that imply stakeholder consequences: 1.0 triggers Board escalation/intervention; 6.0 implies external industry recognition or benchmarking.
