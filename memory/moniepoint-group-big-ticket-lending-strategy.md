## Problem

[[Big-Ticket Lending]] at [[Moniepoint|Moniepoint Inc.]] carries narrow unit economics: treasury cost of funds ~19–20%, book yield 29% reducing balance, leaving ~9% margin to absorb NPL. One default can wipe out portfolio profit. Compounded when borrowers pre-pledge primary assets to other lenders (e.g., [[Alesso]] bikes pledged to [[FCMB]]).

## Strategic Shift (2026)

Move from cash disbursement to **activity-financing**:
- Choose target sectors (oil and gas, cement).
- Finance specific transactions (e.g., PMS purchase from designated supplier).
- Pay suppliers directly — funds tied to purpose.
- Risk appetite is defined by what we choose to finance, not by who we decline.

## Customer Profile

Target is "**tweeners**" — between SME and Corporate, typically served by commercial banking desks. Often lack reliable financial records (created only at loan-application time). Company has evidence SMEs can grow into this segment (e.g., [[Item 7]]: 15M → 4–5M run rate).

## Discipline

- **Single obligor limits**: no ad-hoc violation; exceptions require Board Credit Committee process.
- **Not in the collateral-selling business** — collateral is not the mitigation strategy.
- Accept loss of businesses that don't qualify; competitors chasing with offer letters (some fake) is expected.
