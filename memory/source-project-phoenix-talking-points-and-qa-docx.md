## Key Points

### Part 1 — All-Staff
- **Core message:** "Same mission. Better structure. Bigger reach." Consolidate what we build so we grow into new countries without rebuilding.
- **Structure:** 12-slide arc — Title → What is Phoenix (Before/After) → OpCo vs Platform Co → Moniepoint Group Technology Org → Five Platforms → [[TSP]] Deep Dive → Card Issuance/Acceptance/Payment Gateway → Who Moves vs Who Stays → Team Movements Table → Transition Timeline → What To Do Now → Closing.
- **Anticipated Q&A:** job safety (no reduction), reporting changes (functional unchanged; business-line consolidates under triad), timing (planning Apr 1, ramp Apr 16, delivery by June), salary/benefits (no change), platform preference (accommodated where possible), AI replacement fear (no — enhancement), customer impact (none), Monnify/DD fate (continue, get platform foundations).

### Part 2 — PM & Business Leads
- **Core message:** domain platforms decide WHAT to pay, [[TSP]] decides HOW. Your value shifts upstream (better payment initiation, richer customer context, smarter product logic); downstream execution (routing, fees, posting, rail dispatch) moves to TSP.
- **Structure:** 13 slides — recap → Group tech org → TSP capabilities (4 sub-platforms: Card Switching 42-50% POS, Account Switching/Juliana, VAS 44 integrations, Direct Debit MADD/GoSubscribe/SAFE) → TSP team (65 people from CDD+TPP+NDS) → Card Issuance (boundaries with TSP, Eywa capability IDs) → Card Acceptance (Unwana Enang PTSP/PTAD, Taiwo Ogundipe acquirer) → Payment Gateway/Monnify (26 engineers, Damilare leads, EM vacancy from Vaibhav resignation) → Identity Platform (proposed, details TBD) → Open decisions & risks → Git + AI workflow → Action items.
- **Anticipated Q&A:** team scope shrinkage (value shifts upstream), TSP readiness gate (feature flags — no cutover without TSP capability built+tested+validated), boundary decisions (WHAT vs HOW principle), OKR management during transition (Q1+Q2 current OKRs remain; dual responsibilities Apr-May), external tenant gap (TSP currently internal-callers-only; B2B switching needs architectural home), Direct Debit placement (Frank leans inside TSP — mandate management dependency), tokenisation conflict (dedicated Tokenisation Platform, sequenced early), Git+AI workflow rollout (hands-on session scheduled, AI course on L&D), Juliana Card Switch migration (strangler fig + feature flags; Juliana stays live until TSP proven per transaction type).

### Part 3 — HR / People Management
- **Core message:** no redundancies. Functional reporting unchanged, business-line reporting shifts to platform triads. Contract changes where entity changes (OpCo → DevCo).
- **Structure:** 11 slides — Title → Context & people implications → Group tech org (legal/employment view) → Five platforms with headcount → [[TSP]] people breakdown (65 people, 3 business lines, 2 SRE gaps: CDD has no SRE lead, TPP SRE Chiamaka has no officers) → Card Issuance & Acceptance (both need team builds, Blessing Abel-Oguche for CI, Unwana+Taiwo consolidate for CA) → Payment Gateway people (Vaibhav RESIGNED serving notice, 9 engineers pending reassignment — most urgent action; Ankit Kushwaha acting PM Collections, planned CDD move) → Full team movements table → Role categories moving vs staying → Transition timeline HR milestones → Five concrete HR actions this week.
- **Anticipated Q&A:** contract changes (likely yes for entity changes, legal prepares templates), unwilling movers (surface in 1:1s, accommodate where possible), split teams (manager follows majority, split staff get new business-line manager), Vaibhav situation (promote internal senior, redistribute across Prateek Gupta temporarily, or fast-track external hire — resolve before April ramp), redundancies (none), communication stages (all-staff big picture → PM/leads platform detail → HR 1:1 per affected person).
