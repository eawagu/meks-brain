## Overview

Moniepoint Group MPR Minus Framework is the treasury cost-of-funds model proposed by [[Tosin Eniolorunda]] at the [[Moniepoint 2026 Leadership Retreat UK]] Day 1 to resolve the conflict between Treasury and the Savings business over interest-rate setting.

## Mechanism

- Maximum internal FTP (Funds Transfer Pricing) = MPR (Monetary Policy Rate) − X (e.g., MPR − 6 or MPR − 10).
- The gap between this internal cost-of-funds and the MPR represents treasury revenue.
- Treasury flexibility preserved within the ceiling; no ad-hoc changes tied to market conditions.

## Context

- Current FTB has already changed twice — tied to market conditions rather than algorithmically to MPR.
- Current blended cost of funds: ~8%. Current reverse interest rate: 15%. Space between 17–20% for customer-facing rates.
- Treasury was previously described as "swallowing everything" — constraining business-unit revenue.

## Purpose

- Free [[Savings Strategy]] from treasury-side rate negotiation.
- Establish structural spread locked to monetary policy rather than internal politics.
- Enable AUM-growth experiments with competitive customer rates.
