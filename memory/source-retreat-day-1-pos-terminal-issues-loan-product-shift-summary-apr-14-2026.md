## Summary

Retreat Day 1 strategy session — consumer loan strategy shift from cash loans to lower-risk/lower-yield brand-aligned products (personal loans, BNPL) with phantom 18% cost-of-funds discipline. Deep operational review of POS terminal portfolio: 260% Q-over-Q write-off increase, 880k active MP35Ps, potential sunset cost discussed between $30M and ~50B NBV (conflicting figures). MP35P 3G obsolescence + competitor Ope replacement campaign = urgent strategic problem. Maintenance fee model economically broken ($15 avg repair vs ~$1/subscriber revenue; 160k+ idle refurbished units).

## Key Points

- **Consumer loan plan:** High-volume small-ticket loans; first-year origination ~34.6B ("equivalent to 5 Toby-sized loans"). Scale to 6.3M users Year 2. Cost-of-risk model conservatively 5%/month by end 2027 vs 0.1–0.2% expected for business loans. GLPV ~150B by end 2027; portfolio yield ~7%/month.
- **Strategic product mix shift (Key Decision):** Transition away from cash loans as primary driver — not fully aligned with brand. New mix: personal loans, BNPL, other. Lower risk, lower yield, lower cost.
- **Phantom interest expense (Key Decision):** Include 9.7B phantom line in 2027 P&L (per Bayo). Rationale: financial discipline; prevent aggressive price cuts à la competitors. True profit = stated profit + phantom added back.
- **Collection tactics:** Not adopting aggressive practices (Pompeii-style contact-list shaming). SCCP regulatory environment now stronger. Stance: follow rules, be persistent.
- **Salary product expansion:** Shift from "salary loans" → "employer and employee banking" (payroll services). Dedicated team likely required; extension of [[MoneyBook]].
- **POS Q4 2025 vs Q1 2026:** New terminal purchases down from 730k Q4; 72% positive variance vs budget (not necessarily trend to maintain — may be wiser to buy ahead of price rises). Avg cost/terminal kept at ~$45 via OEM negotiation. ~2B outstanding unpaid logistics bills.
- **Write-offs crisis:** 260% increase Q4→Q1. Age data unclear (not tracked by purchase cohort).
- **Swap economics broken:** New-device swap yields only 0.4% return vs 38% on new activation. Free swaps via insurance yield ~15% best case. Swap volume only 3% down.
- **Maintenance fee model:** 1,500 Naira/subscriber ≈ $1/month revenue. $15 avg repair cost (range $1 to major refurb). 9% revenue dip + 3% expense reduction. 160,000+ refurbished devices idle (unreturned to market).
- **Device allocation flaw:** Q2 revamp priority. Driver shift from BE (Business Efficiency — quality signal) to "capacity to deploy" (past-performance signal). >200k new devices sitting idle. Speaker 9 counter: cap allocations (prior Dapo discussion), not replace BE.
- **Missing devices:** Many deployed-in-system devices physically missing (stockpiling → cannibalization → sold). 40% potential loss. Competitor Topwise-device alignment enables part harvest.
- **MP35P inventory crisis:** Procured ~5M total, ~3.3M deployed. 2.2M MP35P; 1.46M deployed (agents resisting more). Backlogs: 163k refurbished stockpiled, 100k+ hard-to-repair at Magodo center, 176k field-faulty (unlocated), 140k at repair center. 37k Morphon + H9 Pro also undeployed.
- **MP35P 3G obsolescence:** Universal 3G issue. Purchasing continued through mid-last-year; 4-year depreciation → most recent buys have minimal depreciation recognized. Tens of millions USD impact. Options: (1) write-off, (2) motherboard upgrade to 4G (cost TBD), (3) creative redeployment to generate commission revenue.
- **MP35P technical fix:** Solomon's cohort test (50k devices) reduced connection failure rate 1.2%→0.3%; expected to apply across all models.
- **MP35P regulatory angle (Key Decision):** Path depends on CBN engagement. Success → controlled multi-year write-off. Failure → aggressive write-off "big decision."
- **Competitor "Ope" replacement campaign:** Targets existing MoneyPoint terminal locations (not new customers). Demarkets MP35P as obsolete/"illegal" citing new CBN regs. Agent rejection of MP35P primarily driven by this demarketing. Retrieval signals paradoxically advertise replaceable locations to competitors.
- **Agent adoption:** Market feedback — agents will not accept MP35P even free. Forced-mixed portfolio (2 MP35P per 10 terminals) only viable deployment strategy.
- **NBV disagreement:** Speaker 5 worst-case $30M (willing to write off over time); Speaker 9 49-50B Naira. Speaker 8 reconciliation: 880k+ active MP35Ps end of March, 300k+ inactive, partial write-off done, 3.1B remaining inactive NBV, 22B figure context-dependent ("idle/DNS/Unknown"), full sunset = 28B active + 22B inactive ≈ 50B.
