## Summary
Management review meeting deck dated 19 April 2026 covering the Integrated Management System (IMS) at [[TeamApt]] across five ISO standards: [[ISO 20000]] (IT Service Management), [[ISO 22301]] (Business Continuity), [[ISO 27001]] (Information Security), [[ISO 45001]] (Occupational Health & Safety), and [[ISO 9001]] (Quality Management). Reports 83.76% compliance, 15.95% in-progress, 0.28% non-compliance across 351 controls, with 1 major non-conformity (QMS Clause 8.4.1 on external provider evaluations), 48 observations, and 8 opportunities for improvement from the internal audit. External surveillance audit scheduled for 20 April 2026 with [[OPEX]] as consulting partner.

## Key Points
- **IMS compliance snapshot**: 294 compliant (83.76%), 56 in-progress (15.95%), 1 non-compliant (0.28%).
- **Information Security controls maturity**: 73% Managed, 17% Optimized, 8% Defined, 1% Limited, 1% Initial — indicating most controls are recently operating but not yet independently evidenced as optimized.
- **Previous action items closed**: identified NCs/observations from last review closed by 16/04/2026; 2026 surveillance audit scheduled 20/04/2026.
- **Major non-conformity**: QMS Clause 8.4.1 — no evidence of external provider evaluations, assessments, and actions for QMS compliance.
- **8 observations** across IMS clauses 6.1 (risk action effectiveness not validated), 6.3 (OHS change management undefined), 7.2 (training in progress), 7.5.3 (documentation under review), 9.1 (2026 monitoring results not captured), 9.2 (audit not on annual program; 2026 internal audit report pending), 9.3 (management review documented).
- **7 opportunities for improvement (OFI)** spanning IMS Clause 4.4 (role identification evidence), SMS 8.2.6 (CI verification schedule), ISMS A5.3 (segregation of duties for IT), A5.5 (clerical error "NDIC" vs [[NDPC]]), A5.9 (asset inventory gaps for CCTV/biometrics/software), A5.20 (security requirements absent from [[OPEX]] and [[Cybervergent]] SLAs), A6.6 (contracts on [[BambooHR]] not regularly reviewed).
- **Business Impact Analysis**: 7 Critical processes (monitoring, incident resolution, DB cleanup — RPO/RTO 5min–60min), 5 Major, 4 Moderate, 14 Minor.
- **Key information-security risks**: Application Server/DB (Major) — controls include cloud-provider patching, secure-code training, [[Jumpcloud]]; Laptops (Moderate) — [[Filevault]], remote wipe, [[Sentinel]] endpoint; Employee/Training Records — [[HRIS]] with SSO, restricted to People Ops.
- **BCMS risks** include loss of cooling (Minor), integration knowledge gaps (Moderate), prolonged downtime from deployment (Moderate), untested-feature deployment (Moderate).
- **OHS risks** all rated Major across ergonomic, visual health, psychological, electrical, and fire safety — reflects remote-work setup exposure.
- **IMS objectives** include 99.9% service availability, 85% customer satisfaction, 90% on-time project completion, 95% BC training coverage, all targeted by Q1 2026.
- **Service reporting**: 85% customer satisfaction survey result; weekly uptime reports emailed to management every Friday.
- **SWOT (IMS-wide)** highlights cloud resilience, trained staff, banking partnerships as strengths; manpower shortage, over-automation, time-zone fragmentation as weaknesses; CBN/NIBSS/NDPA/PCIDSS non-compliance, SLA breaches, cyberattacks as threats.
- **Asset tooling**: [[Nexus portal]] for computing assets, [[Wiz]] for AWS/GCP cloud assets — Supporting Utilities (CCTV, biometric scanners, software) inventory gap flagged.
- **Change management**: change management desk in place — all changes captured, tracked, reviewed, approved pre-implementation.
