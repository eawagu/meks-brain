## Definition

The **"maybe = reject" framework** is the [[Moniepoint]] hiring framework decision (formalized at the 2026-04-24 [[Head of Engineering hiring]] deliberation): **for senior technical leadership roles, "maybe" candidates are essentially rejects**.

## Reasoning

- The role requires high technical expertise and leadership capability.
- **Ambiguity at this level translates to risk.**
- Going forward, any candidate receiving "maybe" feedback **will not advance to the next stage**.

## Apr 24 application

Candidate B was reclassified from MAYBE to REJECT under this framework, citing solid management experience but vague responses on scaling architecture for international expansion.

Candidate C — still MAYBE — was given a **specialized technical deep-dive within 2 weeks** to either resolve to PROCEED or fall to REJECT under the framework.

## Origin

Referenced in the existing [[Chris Purkis]] page as a calibration rule co-established at the April 1, 2026 batch deliberation. The Apr 24 deliberation hardened it from a calibration rule into an explicit framework decision.

## Why this works structurally

For senior leadership hires, the cost of a wrong-yes (bad senior hire → 6–12 month damage) typically exceeds the cost of a wrong-no (passing on a candidate who would have worked out). The asymmetry justifies a strict reject-on-doubt rule. Aligned with [[Venkatesh Purushothaman]]'s own articulation of the **techno-managerial 80/20 persona** as the senior-EM standard.
