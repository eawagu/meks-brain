Comprehensive organizational restructuring document for [[Project Phoenix]]. Version 1.2, last updated March 31, 2026. Owner: CTO / Project Phoenix Steering Committee.

## Core Change
Transition from product-centric teams to platform-centric teams. Teams organized around core platform capabilities, not products or markets. Market teams become consumers of platform capabilities, not builders.

## 6 Core Platforms
1. **Merchant Acquiring** — POS acceptance, mobile payments, card processing
2. **Digital Banking** — Personal accounts, savings, cards, transfers
3. **Business Banking** — Business accounts, payroll, invoicing
4. **Card Issuance** — Virtual/physical card issuance, card management, tokenization
5. **Payment Processing** — Transaction routing, settlement, reconciliation, orchestration
6. **Infrastructure** — Observability, deployment, security, shared services

## Leadership Structure
Each platform has 3 co-owners: Platform Lead (Engineering), Product Lead, Design Lead. Capability teams of 3-7 engineers are the smallest delivery unit. Cluster Leads coordinate related platforms.

## Transition Timeline (Q2 2026)
- Phase 1 (Apr 1-15): Planning — finalize definitions, assign leads, map teams
- Phase 2 (Apr 16 - May 31): Transition ramp — dual responsibilities, weekly knowledge transfer
- Phase 3 (Jun 1-30): Stabilization — full platform-based delivery

## Platform Company Operating Model
Under Phoenix, group separates into **platform companies** (own IP, build software, charge operating companies) and **operating companies** (own country licenses, customers, sales, compliance). Entities: [[Moniepoint]] MFB (Nigeria), [[TeamApt]] Ltd (Nigeria), Kenya (first international).
