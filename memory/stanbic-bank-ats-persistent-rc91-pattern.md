The [[Stanbic Bank]] ATS route has produced a persistent pattern of RC91 ("Issuer or Switch Inoperative") cycles since 2026-04-03. Each cycle typically self-resolves on the bank side within 10–20 minutes, but the recurrence frequency (~2 cycles/day average, rising) and multi-week duration mark this as a systemic degradation rather than isolated incidents. Cycle 26 on Apr 17 (11:36→11:47 WAT, ~11m) followed the usual bank-resolved profile. Cycle 27 filed at 18:05 WAT Apr 17 by [[Olamide Ajibulu]] in #teamapt-tech-operations crossed the 2h active threshold by the 20:09 WAT tick (2h04m active). Overnight the Slack channel went silent — no resolution signal, no status update — so cycle 27 Slack-confirmed status remains unconfirmed (implicit-resolve consistent with DCIR/Wema pattern). Cycle 28 was reported via email at 10:17 WAT Apr 18 ([[Daniel Armstrong]] → Stanbic team in TDSD-6425 thread, "issue has resurfaced") and bank-resolved ~7 minutes later at 10:24 WAT ([[Godwin Ajiboye]]: "transactions are now processing fine"), with Daniel confirming at 10:43 WAT — typical fast-cycle profile. Cycle 29 surfaced ~4h10m later at 14:52 WAT Apr 18 via the same TDSD-6425 email track ([[Daniel Armstrong]] "issue has resurfaced"), bank-resolved by 14:56 WAT ([[Godwin Ajiboye]] "transactions are processing now") — ~4 minutes end-to-end, the fastest resolution yet in the pattern. At 15:24 WAT Apr 18 Daniel Armstrong posted a follow-up confirmation on the same TDSD-6425 thread ("transactions are processing fine at the moment"), sealing cycle 29 closure ~28 minutes after Godwin's bank-side resolution message. Cycle 30 opened on Apr 18 at 18:33 WAT and resolved at 19:37 WAT — 64 minutes end-to-end, slower than cycles 28 (7m) and 29 (4m) but still within the bank-resolve envelope. Cycle 30 is notable for triple-track visibility: Slack filing by [[Afeez Kazeem]] at 18:39 WAT in #teamapt-tech-operations, email filing at 18:40 WAT to Stanbic's IT Service Management inbox with a reconfirm request at 18:54 WAT, and Jira [[TDSD-6618]] raised at 18:45 WAT (reporter + assignee Afeez Kazeem, Medium priority, Work in progress). Resolution confirmed via email at 19:37 WAT (Afeez: "Transactions are now being processed successfully"). Slack track has not yet posted the resolution — email is authoritative. Four cycles in a 32-hour window (cycle 26 Apr 17 11:36 WAT → cycle 30 Apr 18 19:37 WAT, including the overnight gap) puts the observed cadence at ~3x the historical ~2/day baseline.

The pattern co-occurs with intermittent RC91 on [[NIBSS PTSA — Intermittent RC91 Apr 17]], [[Wema Bank — RC91 P1 Apr 17]], [[UBA Bank — RC91 P1 Apr 17]], [[Polaris Bank]] on [[Sterling + Polaris — Routes Degraded]], reopened [[Ecobank — RC91 on NUS Nodes]] failures (Afeez Apr 18 16:09 WAT direct-to-bank escalation), and the expanded wave captured in [[FCMB — RC91 P1 Apr 17]] and [[Keystone Bank — RC05 P1 Apr 17]] — raising the question of whether a broader upstream authorization-path factor is amplifying otherwise-independent issuer-route noise. No confirmed common cause yet — tracking parallel situations with explicit cross-links. The 18:00–19:00 WAT evening window on Apr 17 produced three new P1 filings within 40 minutes (Stanbic, Polaris, UBA); Apr 18 reproduced the evening-wave pattern with Stanbic cycle 30 (18:33 WAT) + UBA brief (18:47 WAT) landing within 14 minutes.
