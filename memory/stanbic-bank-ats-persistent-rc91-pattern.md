The [[Stanbic Bank]] ATS route has produced a persistent pattern of RC91 ("Issuer or Switch Inoperative") cycles since 2026-04-03. Historical baseline: cycles self-resolve bank-side in 10–20 minutes. Cycle 30 (Apr 18 18:33–19:37 WAT, 64m) already marked a resolution-latency drift. **Cycle 31 escalates that drift into a regime change: 7h3m end-to-end (Apr 19 00:00→07:03 WAT), an order of magnitude longer than any prior cycle in this pattern.** This is the first cycle in the watch window that crossed a multi-hour bank-side resolution boundary, and coincided with a concurrent overnight wave touching Access, NIBSS PTSA, Fidelity, and Union Bank — suggesting the underlying fault surface is no longer cleanly bank-bounded.

Cycle 26 on Apr 17 (11:36→11:47 WAT, ~11m) followed the usual bank-resolved profile. Cycle 27 filed at 18:05 WAT Apr 17 by [[Olamide Ajibulu]] in #teamapt-tech-operations crossed the 2h active threshold by the 20:09 WAT tick (2h04m active). Overnight the Slack channel went silent — no resolution signal, no status update — so cycle 27 Slack-confirmed status remains unconfirmed (implicit-resolve consistent with DCIR/Wema pattern). Cycle 28 was reported via email at 10:17 WAT Apr 18 ([[Daniel Armstrong]] → Stanbic team in TDSD-6425 thread, "issue has resurfaced") and bank-resolved ~7 minutes later at 10:24 WAT ([[Godwin Ajiboye]]: "transactions are now processing fine"), with Daniel confirming at 10:43 WAT — typical fast-cycle profile. Cycle 29 surfaced ~4h10m later at 14:52 WAT Apr 18 via the same TDSD-6425 email track ([[Daniel Armstrong]] "issue has resurfaced"), bank-resolved by 14:56 WAT ([[Godwin Ajiboye]] "transactions are processing now") — ~4 minutes end-to-end, the fastest resolution yet in the pattern. At 15:24 WAT Apr 18 Daniel Armstrong posted a follow-up confirmation on the same TDSD-6425 thread ("transactions are processing fine at the moment"), sealing cycle 29 closure ~28 minutes after Godwin's bank-side resolution message. Cycle 30 opened on Apr 18 at 18:33 WAT and resolved at 19:37 WAT — 64 minutes end-to-end, slower than cycles 28 (7m) and 29 (4m) but still within the bank-resolve envelope. Cycle 30 is notable for triple-track visibility: Slack filing by [[Afeez Kazeem]] at 18:39 WAT in #teamapt-tech-operations, email filing at 18:40 WAT to Stanbic's IT Service Management inbox with a reconfirm request at 18:54 WAT, and Jira [[TDSD-6618]] raised at 18:45 WAT (reporter + assignee Afeez Kazeem, Medium priority, Work in progress). Resolution confirmed via email at 19:37 WAT (Afeez: "Transactions are now being processed successfully").

**Cycle 31 (Apr 19 00:00→07:03 WAT, 7h3m, UNPRECEDENTED):** Overnight cycle opened at 00:00 WAT and did not resolve until 07:03 WAT — **the longest bank-side resolution in the entire pattern**. TDSD-6624 filed 07:18 WAT Apr 19 captures the cycle record. The 7h+ duration is a regime-change signal, not a cadence point: the fault surface now produces multi-hour bank-side latency, materially changing the systemic-degradation framing. Cycle 31 is part of a concurrent overnight wave — same window produced Access cycle 8 (00:00→07:50 WAT, 7h50m, TDSD-6625), NIBSS PTSA (01:25–01:40 WAT, 15m), Fidelity (01:50–02:04 WAT, 14m, bank-resolved), Union Bank (02:40–04:50 WAT, 2h10m, first-time participant on active wave). Five-bank concurrence within ~3h strengthens the upstream-common-mode hypothesis in [[RC91 Multi-Bank Failure Pattern]]. The 23:00–06:00 WAT overnight-delegation window prevented Immediate dispatch during the outage; briefing-2026-04-19 carries this as Decision item.

Five cycles in 44h (cycle 27 Apr 17 18:05 WAT → cycle 31 Apr 19 07:03 WAT). Cadence elevated + resolution-latency regime change.

The pattern co-occurs with intermittent RC91 on prior [[NIBSS PTSA — RC91 Apr 17]] (retired), fresh [[NIBSS PTSA — RC91 Apr 19]], [[Wema Bank — RC91 P1 Apr 17]], [[UBA Bank — RC91 P1 Apr 17]], [[Polaris Bank]] on [[Sterling + Polaris — Routes Degraded]], reopened [[Ecobank — RC91 on NUS Nodes]] failures, and the expanded wave captured in [[FCMB — RC91 P1 Apr 17]], [[Keystone Bank — RC05 P1 Apr 17]], [[Fidelity Bank ATS — RC91 Failure Ongoing]], and [[Union Bank — RC91 P1 Apr 19]] — raising the question of whether a broader upstream authorization-path factor is amplifying otherwise-independent issuer-route noise. No confirmed common cause yet — tracking parallel situations with explicit cross-links. The 18:00–19:00 WAT evening windows on Apr 17 and Apr 18 both produced multi-bank P1 filings within minutes; Apr 19 overnight wave is the largest concurrence yet observed.
