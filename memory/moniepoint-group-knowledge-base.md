## Summary

Comprehensive internal knowledge base documenting TeamApt's transformation from a bank software services provider (founded June 2015) into the Moniepoint payment infrastructure group. Covers the company's founding by Tosin Eniolorunda and Felix Ike, the DCIR (Direct Card Issuer Routing) innovation that made MoniePoint terminals dominant, regulatory compliance challenges, Juliana Switch as compliance-compliant evolution, unicorn status ($1B valuation, $110M Series C, Oct 2024), National MFB upgrade (Jan 2026), and Project Phoenix platformization strategy.

## Key Points

- Founded June 2015 as TeamApt (bank software); rebranded January 2023 to Moniepoint Inc as parent holding
- DCIR was competitive advantage (shortened transaction path) but became regulatory gap — required Juliana Switch consolidation and CBN certification (Afrigo, Verve, Visa, Mastercard, NIBSS)
- Unicorn status achieved October 2024 ($1B valuation, $110M Series C led by Google Africa Investment Fund)
- National MFB status upgrade January 2026 (expanded scope from Unit to all 36 states + FCT)
- Project Phoenix: group-wide platformization + AI-nativeness (Eywa) to enable multi-country expansion without code duplication
- TeamApt Ltd holds critical CBN licenses (Switching & Processing, PTSP, SuperAgent, Non-Bank Acquiring) that make MoniePoint's POS business legally viable
- MFB-operated systems (CMS Manager, IRIS, Atlas) remain blindspots in Project Phoenix planning
