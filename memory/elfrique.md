## Overview
Elfrique Solutions Limited is a Nigerian platform providing events, contests, flights, hotels, tours, and trivia services. The company is owned by Mek's father. [[Emeka Awagu]] (Mek) serves as a Director and is the developer building the new version of the site.

## Legal Identity
- **Full legal name:** Elfrique Solutions Limited
- **Domain:** elfrique.com

## Directors
- **[[Emeka Awagu]]** — Director (also lead developer on the new site)
- **[[Mordi, Joe Ebube]]** — Executive Director (commercial and operational)

## Platform Features
- **Events & Ticketing** — physical and online events with ticket sales, QR code check-in
- **Contests & Voting** — pageant voting and awards contests with configurable voting controls
- **Flights** — flight booking (not yet demoed on new site)
- **Hotels** — hotel booking (not yet demoed on new site)
- **Tours** — tour listings (not yet demoed on new site)
- **Trivia** — trivia contests (not yet demoed on new site)

## New Site Development
Mek is building the new Elfrique site. The Apr 9, 2026 product meeting covered a demo of the **events and contests features** only — other features (flights, hotels, tours, trivia) will follow in subsequent demos.

## Technical Details
- Current hosting: [[InMotion]]
- Planned hosting: [[Azure]] (migration under assessment)
- Payment gateway: [[Flutterwave]] (single web group for callbacks across old and new site)

## Legal History
- **Elfrique Solutions Ltd v [[Optisoft Technology Co. Ltd]]** — breach of contract action over a failed 2021 website-restructuring / travel-platform engagement. Suit filed 25 Aug 2022; judgment delivered 31 March 2026 by [[Hon. Justice Efunkumbi]]. Elfrique recovered ₦300,000 mobilization refund plus 10% p.a. interest from 6 Dec 2021 until liquidation, plus ₦250,000 costs. Larger claims (₦12.15M loss of revenue, ₦5M general damages) refused under the *Hadley v Baxendale* reasonable-contemplation rule. Counsel: [[Ben Onuora & Co]] (Ngozi Onuora lead). See [[Elfrique Solutions v Optisoft Technology — Judgment (31 Mar 2026)]] and [[Breach of Contract — Nigerian Damages Framework]].
