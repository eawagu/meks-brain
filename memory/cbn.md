[[CBN]] (Central Bank of Nigeria) is the primary financial sector regulator in Nigeria. Requires near-real-time (2–3 hour) cyber incident reporting from banks and oversees critical information infrastructure designations. Approval is required for core banking changes at licensed institutions.

## Regulatory Exposure — TeamApt IMS
Per the [[TeamApt Management Review 19_04_2026]], non-compliance with CBN requirements is explicitly flagged as a major IMS threat — 'Failure of the organisation to comply with CBN, NIBSS, NDPR, PCI DSS and other requirements leading to sanctions and loss of operating licences' is rated Major in the Service Management Risk register. Controls include a procedure for identification of legal/regulatory/contractual requirements and a Legal Register.

## DDMFB 2026 IT Strategy — Missing Alignment
Per [[DDMFB_2026_IT_Strategy_Review]], CBN circulars on technology risk management, outsourcing, and cloud computing for financial institutions are not referenced in DavoDani Microfinance Bank's 2026 IT Strategy document — flagged as a critical gap. Additionally, CBN approval timeline for the proposed [[Core Banking System]] migration (typically 12–24 months) was not addressed, and CBN guidelines on automated financial communications were not considered in the [[Customer Experience Platform]] section.
