## Definition

The **technical depth bar** is the [[Moniepoint]] hiring framework decision (Apr 24 HoE deliberation) that defines the minimum technical-depth requirement for senior technical leadership roles.

## Bar (must-haves)

1. **Hands-on technical depth in relevant domains — minimum 10 years.**
2. **Proven experience scaling systems.**
3. **Leadership of large engineering teams.**

## Strong preferences

- Experience in fintech or payments.
- Demonstrated ability to modernize legacy systems.
- AI/ML exposure.

## Assessment method

- Deep technical case-study discussions.
- Architectural decision walkthrough.
- Behavioural questions about team leadership and difficult-situation handling.

## Process refinements

- **Pre-screen** verifiable technical contributions (GitHub, published architecture docs).
- **Focus on hands-on architectural decisions** rather than theoretical frameworks.
- **Mandatory "war story" section** — candidate describes complex technical challenges they personally resolved.
- **Standardized rubric** across interviewers.

## Apr 24 application

Candidate A was rejected for failing the bar: 20+ years of industry experience but provided theoretical frameworks without concrete implementation details when asked about architecture decisions.
