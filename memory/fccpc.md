## Overview

FCCPC (Federal Competition and Consumer Protection Commission) is the Nigerian competition regulator.

## Scope Constraint

- **CBN carve-outs** constrain FCCPC's mandate over payments and banking — the Central Bank of Nigeria holds primary authority over systemic financial stability.
- Central Bank prioritizes systemic stability over consumer-competition enforcement in payments.

## Moniepoint Posture

- Educate regulators on systemic risks, capital adequacy, foreclosure of demand, monopoly concerns.
- Pursue legal reforms rather than regulatory arbitrage.
- **Predatory pricing watch**: 0% commercial bank offers currently ~0.3–0.4% acquirer share (from 0.2%). If a bank hits ~10% share, dominance/cross-subsidy concerns would apply.
