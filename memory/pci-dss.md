## Definition

**PCI-DSS** (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard) is a security framework administered by the PCI Security Standards Council that defines technical and operational requirements for any system that stores, processes, or transmits cardholder data.

It is mandatory for card-handling platforms regardless of jurisdiction.

## Phoenix CI&P scope

The [[Card Issuance and Processing]] platform under [[Project Phoenix]] must satisfy PCI-DSS requirements as part of its multi-jurisdictional regulatory footprint, alongside:

- African banking regulations
- Local payment scheme rules (Visa, Mastercard, Verve)
- UK and EU requirements (PSD2-compliant SCA, etc.) for [[MonieWorld]] tenant

## 2026-04-27 — CI&P Kick Off ownership

At the 2026-04-27 CI&P kick-off, **regulatory alignment** — including PCI-DSS — was assigned to [[Ketan Dhamasana]]. His action item: "Ensure regulatory compliance and alignment with all applicable jurisdictional requirements."
