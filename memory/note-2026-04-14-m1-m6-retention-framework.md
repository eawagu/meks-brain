## Summary

Captured note on Apr 14, 2026 at 09:31 UTC. Frames M1–M6 retention as a monthly cohort metric — percent of acquisition-month users still active at each subsequent month. Provides benchmarks by vertical (B2B SaaS, FinTech, Consumer, E-commerce/marketplaces), curve shape diagnostics (healthy flattening vs. no-floor decline vs. structural drop-off), and FinTech-specific application — minimum activation event predicting M6 retention as the product intervention locus.

## Key Points

- **Definition:** M0 is the acquisition month (100% baseline); M1–M6 measures the percent of that cohort still active in each subsequent month.
- **Benchmarks:** B2B SaaS retains best (M1 70–85%, M6 55–70%); FinTech payments/lending/wallets in the middle (M1 50–70%, M6 30–50%); Consumer apps worst (M1 25–40%, M6 5–15%); E-commerce/marketplaces between consumer and FinTech.
- **Enterprise > SMB** on retention. Most consumer churn happens in the first week.
- **Curve shape diagnostics** — three canonical shapes:
  - Steep early drop then flattening → retained core exists; invest in onboarding. Healthy.
  - Steady decline that never flattens → no retention floor; product lacks habit loop or switching cost. Red flag.
  - Drop-off at a specific month → structural cause (contract end, seasonal, UX failure). Diagnosable and fixable.
- **Flattening = plateau = product-market fit** for a segment. M3→M6 still declining steeply means no habitual use.
- **FinTech strategic question:** what's the minimum activation event that predicts M6 retention? That locus is where product intervention belongs — CAC is already sunk after M1.
