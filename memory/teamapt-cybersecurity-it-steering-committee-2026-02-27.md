## Summary

TeamApt Management Cybersecurity & IT Steering Committee meeting on February 27, 2026. Reviewed performance metrics (98.75% PTSA success rate, 99.99% account switch success, 99.57% modified disbursement), AWS Outpost migration progress for data sovereignty compliance, and two critical security vulnerabilities: deprecated TLS 1.0/1.1 on teamapp.com requiring C-suite approval for Cloudflare changes, and hardcoded weak secrets enabling access to 8,000 customer accounts during testing.

## Key Points

- PTSA: 98.75% success rate, 88.83% approval rate, 142s avg response time for 2025
- Account switch: 99.99% success rate, 98.68% approval rate
- Modified disbursement improved from 96% (2024) to 99.57% (2025)
- AWS Outpost migration for data sovereignty; account switch cutover anticipated next week; core switch migration Q2 2026
- Infrastructure as Code implemented on AWS; resource creation rights revoked from direct access
- Harness (SDLC tool) migration 86% complete on dev/staging
- Zero security incidents in 3 months; one critical external researcher finding
- Critical: deprecated TLS 1.0/1.1 on teamapp.com — Cloudflare config change needs C-suite approval due to third-party connectivity risk
- Highly critical: hardcoded passwords enabling brute force access; testing found evidence of access to 8,000 customer accounts and their data
- ISO 27001 audit scheduled April; mock audit in March (SLA approval pending)
- PCIDSS assessment: internal review underway; auditors arriving April
- VAPT vendor consideration: switching from MWR to KPMG
- Information security training: 86 of 219 staff completed (deadline March 31)
- Threat hunting exercise H1 2026 ongoing; AWS SIEM integration in progress
