## Summary

Day 1 retreat session covering two topics: (1) Consumer-side lending strategy pivot toward small/frequent loans and BNPL, with a reframing from "salary lending" to comprehensive [[Employer Employee Banking]]; (2) POS terminal fleet crisis — rising write-offs, idle inventory, MP35P 3G obsolescence, competitor demarketing pressure (notably [[OPay]]), and a proposed capacity-based allocation model for deployment.

## Key Points — Consumer Lending

- **Strategy pivot**: small ticket 30/60/90-day loans targeting repeat users (projected 3–4 loans/customer/year).
- **Year-one exposure capped at ₦34.6B** — small pilot, scale next year after risk validation.
- **Product mix**: reduce reliance on cash loans; introduce [[BNPL]] and other lower-risk/lower-yield products.
- **P&L outlook**: small 2026 loss, larger 2027 profit at 18 cost-of-funds assumption (excl. CAC).
- **End-2027 GLPV target**: just under ₦150B at ~7% monthly yield.
- **Risk posture**: 5% monthly cost of risk by end-2027 (conservative); 0.1–0.2% for prospective business loans.
- **Phantom interest expense**: ₦9.7B in 2027 — used as pricing discipline to prevent price crashes.
- **Scaling plan**: expand to all Moniepoint users (~6.3M next year) after controlling risk.
- **Collections posture**: follow rules, persistent but compliant. Market anecdotes of aggressive foreign lenders dismissed as often sensationalized/seeded; internal stance rejects unethical practices.
- **Platform readiness**: credit side advanced; launch contingent on complete [[Account Management Strategy]].
- **Salary lending reframe**: scope expanded to employer-and-employee banking including payroll — two tracks: existing salary recipients + enabling merchants to run payroll on Moniepoint.

## Key Points — POS Fleet

- **Procurement**: Q4'25 730K devices → Q1'26 reduced to curb arbitrary buying. Memory prices expected to rise; consider advance buys.
- **Unit cost**: avg new terminal ~$45; target $1–$2 reduction. ~₦2B unpaid logistics bills in process.
- **Write-offs up ~260%** vs Q4'25. Need better device-cohort tracking.
- **Revenue profile**: activation +6%, refunds -62%; swaps erode economics (new-device swaps ~0.4% vs ~38% activation; insurance can zero out swap revenue). Maintenance revenue ₦1,500/BO down 9% against ~$15/device cost.
- **Idle inventory crisis**: 160K+ refurbished devices not redeployed; 200K+ new devices idle; ~664K total idle in DNS.
- **MP35P 3G limitation**: discontinue or upgrade motherboards to 4G on repair. 163K repaired MP35 undeployed. ~176K faulty in DNS, >140K at repair centers. Technical fix reduced failures 1.7% → 0.3% but market perception still penalizes 3G.
- **MP35 fleet totals** (figures disputed): ~5M ever procured, ~3.3M deployed; MP35D ~2.2M total (~1.46M deployed). NBV dispute: 22B vs ~50B — consolidation action item.
- **Allocation model proposal**: use capacity-to-deploy as cap; keep BE as buffer/priority — not quantity driver. Monthly caps based on prior deployment; co-location to support pickups.
- **Anti-gaming**: concern about proxy accounts inflating deployments; cross-unit collaboration + caps planned.
- **Device loss diagnosis**: past 200K loss from cannibalization (e.g., Topwise parts), unauthorized sales, disposals without returned funds. ~40% loss assumption. New controls: limit stockpiling + disposal.
- **Financial options on MP35P**: immediate write-off; invest in 4G upgrade; free-give to drive commissions. Models pending.
- **Regulatory strategy**: engage regulators to curb competitors' demarketing (notably [[OPay]] claiming 3G/Android 5 illegal) and enable a controlled 4-year MP35 write-off.
- **Competitive reality**: device retrievals for qualification issues don't deter competitor replacement; need proactive retention outreach. Worst-case ~$30M impact potentially absorbable over time.
- **Facilities strain**: repair centers (e.g., Magodo) overwhelmed; storage pressure growing; larger space requests active.
- **FMCG-style push-to-remote-areas rejected**: terminals are relationship/retention assets, not interchangeable SKUs.

## Key Decisions

- Launch blocked if [[Account Management Strategy]] incomplete.
- Reframe from "salary lending" to full [[Employer Employee Banking]] incl. payroll.
- Maintain disciplined procurement, consider advance buys to hedge component price increases.
- Pursue regulatory intervention to enable controlled 4-year MP35 write-off.
- Follow compliant collections rules — no unethical tactics.
- Retrieval alone won't prevent competitor replacement — plan accordingly.
