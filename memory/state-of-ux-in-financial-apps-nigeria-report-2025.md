## Summary

A comprehensive UX analysis by [[Interswitch]] of 10 leading Nigerian financial apps, combining heuristic evaluations against Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics, surveys of 342 participants, and 35 in-depth interviews. The report identifies industry strengths (system status visibility, error prevention) and critical gaps (help/documentation, recognition over recall, system-real world match).

## Key Points

- **Apps evaluated**: [[Sterling Bank]], [[Access Bank]], [[UBA]], [[OPay]], [[First Bank]], [[Zenith Bank]], [[GTBank]], [[Providus]], [[Moniepoint]], [[Kuda]]
- **Heuristic strengths**: Visibility of System Status (90%), Error Prevention (89%), Flexibility & Efficiency (71%)
- **Heuristic weaknesses**: Help & Documentation (20%), Recognition Rather Than Recall (38%), Match Between System & Real World (46%)
- **Task performance**: Transfers (68% avg pass), Purchasing Airtime (62%), Paying Bills (53%) — all moderate, indicating room for improvement
- **User survey**: 91% find app language understandable; 74% feel informed about transaction status; 87% find transfers easy; 80% can locate features easily
- **Critical gap**: 71% of Nigerians find help options on financial apps ineffective; 57% find error messages clear
- **User desires**: Personalized dashboards, tap-and-pay, multi-currency wallets, virtual cards, crypto/investment features, faster transactions, better customer support
- **Industry trends**: Super apps, AI-driven hyper-personalization, embedded finance, open banking, alternative payment solutions
- **Internal stakeholder insights**: Stakeholder resistance to UX testing is a recurring challenge; tight delivery timelines hinder proper UX work; product managers need to balance business goals with user needs
- **Actionable recommendations**: Minimal interfaces, AI-driven personalization, optimized customer support, expanding financial features, security-UX balance, real-time notifications, regular usability testing
