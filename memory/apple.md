## Overview

Apple is a technology company that designs and develops its own operating systems, hardware, application software, and services. Its defining competitive advantage is ownership of the full product stack — from silicon to OS to applications — enabling tighter integration than competitors who rely on third-party components at multiple layers.

## Business Strategy

Per [[Apple's_key_business_objectives]], Apple's strategy rests on four pillars:

1. **Vertical integration** — simultaneous ownership of OS, hardware, app software, and services yields innovative design, superior ease-of-use, and seamless integration that third-party ecosystems cannot replicate
2. **Platform ecosystem** — Digital Content and Services platform spans iOS, Mac, Apple Watch, Apple TV apps, digital content, and books; accessible across all Apple device categories and Windows PCs
3. **Third-party ecosystem** — supports community of third-party hardware/software developers and digital content creators to extend platform value
4. **Retail and distribution** — high-quality buying experience with knowledgeable salespersons treated as a deliberate competitive lever, not just a sales channel; expanding own retail/online stores and third-party distribution network
