## Summary

Day 1 afternoon session of the Moniepoint 2026 Leadership Retreat UK covering Savings strategy pivot, cost-of-funds framework, 2026 targets for Card and Acquiring businesses, customer-experience metric redefinition, cost governance via Monthly Performance Reviews, and mandated presentation standards (MECE + OKR format).

## Key Points — Presentation Standards

- **[[MECE Framework]]** (McKinsey: Mutually Exclusive, Comprehensively Exhaustive) established as the required structure for all presentations. [[Tosin Eniolorunda]] to circulate the document.
- **[[OKR Presentation Standard]]** mandated: every presentation must state Objectives aligned with the 4 company goals, Key Results, and Initiatives.
- **Four company goals** (anchors for all OKRs): be a product people love; drive revenue; manage operations/risk/compliance; manage costs.
- "Sustainable market leadership" OKR paused for most departments due to unreliable market-share measurement; Card business retains it because data is reliable.

## Key Points — Savings

- **Penetration**: 9.9% (vs 9% target). New internal target: 20% by end of 2026.
- **Business customer penetration**: 19.3% (~1 in 5 businesses).
- **AUM shortfall**: ~₦3B behind — offline sales launch delayed; Q1 offline contribution ~₦30B unrealized.
- **Monthly AUM plan**: Jan→Feb 67→82; Feb→Mar 82→106; Mar→Apr 106→135; Apr→May 135→167 — flagged "governance failure" (actual March 102 vs target 106).
- **Strategy pivot**: product-centric → customer-centric. Cannot compete on highest rates; compete on habit/advisory UX.
- **Portfolio**: [[Target Savings]] (breakout, launched Nov 2025), [[Flexible Savings]] (lagging — no PM), [[Fixed Savings]] (blocked/low), [[SAYT]] (~800K users post-cleanup, cyclical).
- **[[Business Owner Account]]** decommissioned for new SAYT customers (legacy users to be migrated).
- **Primary success metric** shifted from AUM → **Revenue** per [[Tosin Eniolorunda]] directive.
- **[[MPR Minus Framework]]** proposed: treasury max cost-of-funds = MPR − X (e.g., MPR−6); locks in a structural spread that represents treasury revenue.
- Current blended cost of funds: ~8%. Potential customer rate ceiling ~17–20%.
- **[[Ajo Esusu Offline Savings]]**: collector-contributor model; Q2 launch target at risk (PM + designer gap).
- **[[Personalization Studio]]**: Q3 through year-end — configurable financial products.

## Key Points — Card Business 2026 (Tosin's presentation)

- **2025 recap**: 7.97T transactions processed; 13.3M cards sold; market share 5.9→11.7%; position 7th→3rd (behind OP and UBA).
- **Card economics**: cost ₦1,500, sold ₦1,000 — distribution subsidy. ₦2.2B of the ₦13.3B card sales funded customer acquisition.
- **2026 targets**: 23M cards; 1.3B transactions; #2 market position.
- **Product roadmap**: tap-to-pay (card + mobile), virtual cards, business cards.
- **March Q1 anomaly**: stopped card sales to business customers to improve transaction quality; March Mastercard back-billing inflated costs.
- **CBN ATM rule**: 1 ATM per 7,500 cards issued — significant cost/operational burden flagged.
- **Revenue vs. cost**: transaction revenue (interchange) outpacing scheme fees; web transaction mix yields higher revenue.

## Key Points — Acquiring Business 2026 (Solomon's presentation)

- **2025**: ₦11B revenue (24% YoY); ~51% gross value; 94.8% backend success rate; P95 latency 32s; 0.08% complaints.
- **2025 issues**: scheme fee inflation, competition, MySQL incidents, engineering/product leadership gaps.
- **2026 targets**: ₦247B revenue (17% growth); 49% gross margin; 97% backend success rate; 20s P95.
- **[[Customer-Facing Success Rate]]** is the primary metric (currently 91%, steady) — not backend. Backend for internal use only.
- **HTTP query verification**: replacing ISO process; 30s countdown to customer; KR3 target 0.01%.
- **[[Push to Card]]** proactive-refund strategy: plug into Settlement/Recon to refund within 24h of settlement (avg customer reports to bank in 3–4 days).
- **Disputes**: dropped 30%→9% in Q1; target <5%, north star <1–2%.
- **Q1 numbers**: revenue actual ₦53.4B vs ₦62.4B target (speaker misstated as "ahead"); margin 64.4% vs 61% target.

## Key Points — International Expansion & Compliance

- Full coverage target: Mastercard, Visa, Amex, others. International card $1M stretch (not in official budget).
- **[[Geofencing Compliance]]** SDK built into POS app; certified with NIBSS ahead of schedule. Capability-ready for CBN enforcement; geolocation accuracy ±20m limits tight-radius enforcement. Risk: POS terminals operating in China/Russia/UK.
- Compliance SLA target: <7 days (from ~1 month).

## Key Points — Customer Experience / Governance

- **Complaint rate**: 0.08% → 0.04% Q1; year-end goal 0.01%. Must be normalized against active customers for true trend.
- **[[Active Customer Definition]]** currently "one outbound transaction" — flagged as inaccurate; must be fixed.
- **[[Monthly Performance Reviews]]** (MPRs) instituted for all business units starting next month; review cost views (COGS, promotions, commissions) plus business metrics. Response to CSAT + cost management gaps.
- **CSAT** elevated to first OKR for 2026.
- **PAT**: single-digit today on strong revenue — cost control across COGS and OPEX required. Path to 20% PAT.
- **Incident resolution**: <20min Q1; target <5–10min. Layer caching work key enabler.

## Key Decisions

- Re-evaluate static interest-rate approach; 8% blended cost of funds suggests room for competitive customer rates.
- BO account decommissioned for new SAYT customers.
- Active-customer definition must be fixed.
- Savings success metric: AUM → Revenue.
- 2026 card business targets set: 23M cards, 1.3B txn, #2 position.
- Customer-facing success rate is the primary reported metric for Acquiring.
- MPRs instituted for all BUs starting next month.
- Timmy's presentation postponed to Day 2 for adequate time.
