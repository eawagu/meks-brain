## Definition

**AI-native company strategy** is [[Moniepoint]]'s stated transition into a fully AI-native organization — articulated by [[Emeka Awagu]] in the 2026-04-24 Head of Engineering Round 2 interview with [[Venkatesh Purushothaman]].

## Phasing

- **First cohorts**: product, engineering, and design.
- **12-month goal**: AI integrated into entire company workflows, **including finance and HR**.

## Architecture pattern

- AI covers product management → engineering specs → deployment.
- AI handles: creating artifacts, translating product chain specs into engineering specs, deployment.
- **Humans do not directly interact with code or Jira** in the future state.
- **Human oversight via "gates"** to ensure correct architecture / code.

## Current state

- **NOT YET running fully autonomous indefinite agents** — early stages.
- **Immediate focus**: developing the **"harness" around the AI** to ensure it performs as expected. **Majority of current work** is on this harness. See [[Moniepoint AI harness]].

## Why this matters

The strategy implies that **scaling is decoupled from headcount in a fundamental way** — if AI handles 40–50% of code and 20–35% of code reviews (per [[Venkatesh Purushothaman]]'s Circle.ai data), team-size economics shift toward smaller-but-more-leveraged squads. This is the operational mechanism behind "country launches in weeks instead of months" (see [[Moniepoint country-launch tech debt]]).
