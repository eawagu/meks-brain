## Overview

International Expansion is Moniepoint's strategic framework for multi-country growth beyond its Nigerian base. Enabled by [[Project Phoenix]]'s [[Platform Strategy]] ("One Platform, Many Markets") — a new country is a configuration profile, not a new codebase.

## Current Markets

- **Nigeria** — base market, largest operation
- **UK** — diaspora remittances and business banking
- **Kenya** — mobile money, entering via [[Sumac Microfinance Bank]] acquisition (~three-year deal close). See [[Kenya Launch]].

## Market-Entry Model

**License-led M&A** — Kenya establishes this as the template: acquire a licensed entity in the target market rather than pursuing greenfield licensing. Rationale: faster market entry, regulatory credibility, operational base.

## Post-Kenya Strategy

- **Class Strategy** framework in development by a new hire, due upon June resumption (after the platformization change freeze). See [[Class Strategy]].
- **Candidate markets:** South Africa, Morocco, Kenya (current), DRC — the latter flagged as Nigeria-like margin opportunity (polarized, high-margin).
- **Market sizing logic:** Smaller markets can offer lower revenue but better margins + less competition.

## Required Platform Capabilities

- Multi-currency support
- Regulatory compliance engine (per-country)
- Cross-border routing
