## Summary

Certified True Copy of judgment delivered 31 March 2026 by [[Hon. Justice Efunkumbi]] in the matter of [[Elfrique|Elfrique Solutions Limited]] (Claimant) v [[Optisoft Technology Co. Ltd]] (Defendant). Action arose from a failed 2021 software engagement — website restructuring and travel/tours platform development. Court found for Claimant on the mobilization refund and costs, and refused loss-of-revenue and general-damages claims applying the *Hadley v Baxendale* reasonable-contemplation test. See [[Breach of Contract — Nigerian Damages Framework]].

## Ratio Decidendi

**Contract formed.** Offer, acceptance, and consideration established by documentary evidence — Exhibits C2A–C2E (initial proposal), C3–C4 (email confirmation of terms), C5 (project tracker), C6 (payment schedule), and C7–C8 (payment evidence).

**Breach established on Defendant's side.** Defendant failed to commence within the agreed 10-week timeline, unilaterally proposed revising cost from ₦1.75M to ₦4.5M, and offered to refund the mobilization but never did. Court noted Defendant's professional failing — quoting before inspecting the technical stack: *"How could you quote for a job that you have not examined the system to know exactly what you are dealing with? Why would you take the Claimant's say-so without first being sure for yourself? You are the technical expert after all?"*

**Damages limited per Hadley v Baxendale.** Court applied the reasonable-contemplation rule — aggrieved party recovers only the loss that was reasonably foreseeable at the time of contract. Loss-of-revenue (travel/tours platform, voting feature, vendor management) and general-damages claims failed this test. Mobilization fee returns as restitution for work not done. See [[Breach of Contract — Nigerian Damages Framework]].
