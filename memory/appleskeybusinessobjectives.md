## Summary

Apple's key business objectives center on delivering the best user experience through tight vertical integration of hardware, software, and services. The strategy leverages Apple's unique ability to own its full stack — OS, hardware, applications, and services — to produce products with innovative design, ease-of-use, and seamless integration. Retail and third-party distribution are explicitly part of the strategy, treated as levers for customer acquisition and post-sales support quality.

## Key Points

- Core commitment: best user experience through innovative hardware, software, and services
- Competitive advantage: unique ability to design and develop its own OS, hardware, application software, and services simultaneously
- Platform expansion: Digital Content and Services platform covering iOS, Mac, Apple Watch, Apple TV apps, digital content, and books
- Access devices: Mac, Windows PCs, iPhone, iPad, iPod touch, Apple TV, Apple Watch
- Third-party ecosystem: supports community of third-party hardware/software developers and digital content creators
- Retail strategy: high-quality buying experience with knowledgeable salespersons as a deliberate competitive lever
- Distribution: expanding own retail/online stores and third-party distribution network to reach more customers
