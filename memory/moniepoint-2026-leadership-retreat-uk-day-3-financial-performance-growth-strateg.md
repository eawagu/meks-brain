## Profitability Problem

**Current PAT margin: 8%. Target: mid-30s (peer parity).**

Key cost drivers:
- **Extreme promos** — ~₦140B on promos in one account; systemic risk driven by market distortions (regulators should fix)
- **Verve pricing** — regulatory arbitrage to raise prices anti-competitively; Moniepoint cannot counter
- **Operational inefficiencies** — lack of cost-consciousness across COGS, OPEX, commissions, promos

### Cost Areas for Omondi's Plan
- Promo rewards
- Commission structures
- Cloud costs
- Budget caps
- Credit loss
- Depreciation

Reinvest those profits into growth in new countries or Nigeria. Efficiency drive must not sacrifice growth (digital businesses, WC, product penetration).

## FTP-Based Financial Reporting (New)

### Granularity
- Classify by profit center (business vs personal)
- Deposit sources broken down: card business, account training business, Adriana, Money Bonds
- View toggle: with FTP vs without FTP

### FTP Explanation
- **FTP (Funds Transfer Pricing)** — "audit rate" compensating deposit-holders and charging asset-holders
- Can influence business behavior: lower FTP for a department incentivizes lower-rate lending; higher FTP forces selectivity on loan profitability

### Example Allocations
- **POS depreciation (98% of fixed assets)** — allocated only to businesses using them: Card payments, POS transfer line within Account Payments
- **FTP benefit** to deposit-gathering houses: 30% of MPR
- **FTP charge** to asset-creating houses: MPR − 6

### Practical View
- **Adrian/Savings (without FTP)** — loss of 2.9 (interest expense + salaries)
- **Adrian/Savings (with FTP)** — 3.7 benefit — low-cost funds gathering recognized
- Lending unit profit reduced by "FTP expense" line in the inverse case

### Rollout
- Reporting accessible to leaders
- Dedicated review session next week
- Structure may change, principles stable
- Described as "beginning of a new era"
