## Summary

[[Mek]]'s review of the Technology Excellence & Operational Performance section (15% weight) of the [[TeamApt]] MD/CEO KPI Scorecard FY2026. Evaluated for realism given Moniepoint's operating context as a payments and switching company. Proposes two concrete rubric revisions (uptime and SLA compliance) and leaves the cybersecurity rubric unchanged. Flags that the 15% technology weight — and CTO footprint of ~11% — structurally positions technology as a utility rather than product.

## Key Points

- **Org structure context:** CTO ([[Mek]]) owns Platform Uptime & Reliability + Operational Efficiency & SLA Compliance (11% of CEO scorecard). Cybersecurity (4%) flows to someone else. Product Innovation Pipeline (6%, Strategic Growth section) flows to [[Chief Product Officer]]. Platform modernisation / [[Project Phoenix]] flows to a separate Platform Organisation. Business lines consume services from the platform org.
- **Revision 1 — Platform Uptime & Reliability (6% weight):** Raise "Meets Expectations" from 99.9% to 99.95% and shift full scale upward. Rationale: 99.9% (~8.75 hours downtime/year) is too soft for switching infrastructure banks depend on. [[Monnify]] disbursements already at 99.99% success; [[Atlas]] approaches 500M txn/month; [[Juliana Switch]] serves issuing banks. 99.9% would fall below issuing-bank contractual expectations. New scale: 1.0 <98% | 2.0 98–99.9% | 3.0 ≥99.95% | 4.0 ≥99.97% | 5.0 ≥99.99% | 6.0 ≥99.995%.
- **Revision 2 — Operational Efficiency & SLA Compliance (5% weight):** Add per-category floor requirement at every rating level. Rationale: composite bundling of client onboarding + transaction processing + support resolution can mask a failing domain (e.g., txn processing 99% + onboarding 75% averages to a "pass" while hiding a real problem). New floors: 3.0 no category <80% | 4.0 no category <85% | 5.0 no category <92% | 6.0 no category <97%.
- **Revision 3 — Cybersecurity Posture (4% weight):** UNCHANGED. Rubric is well-calibrated. Weight at 4% is defensible for a non-fund-holding infrastructure company, though 5% would better reflect scheme compliance exposure ([[Mastercard]], [[Verve]], [[Afrigo]]). Not the hill to die on.
- **Observations (not formal revisions):** 15% total technology weight in a technology infrastructure company is low but acceptable if Board views technology as enabler, not product. CTO's actual footprint (~11%) is a keep-the-lights-on mandate with no build/evolve component — by design given Platform Org + CPO structure. CEO scorecard structurally treats technology as a utility at "meets expectations."
