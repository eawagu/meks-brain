## Definition

The **Organizational Framework** is [[Moniepoint]]'s mental model for designing and operating teams, introduced at the [[Moniepoint 2026 Leadership Retreat UK]] by [[Tosin Eniolorunda]]. Every leader is expected to apply it personally, not as a company-wide abstraction.

## The Seven Elements

1. **Goals** — explicit, cascaded objectives. If a goal is not defined, it does not exist.
2. **Structure** — organizational units aligned to goals; clear ownership, decision authority, and accountability. Structure serves goals, not individuals.
3. **People** — team members evaluated by the [[Eight Judgment Traits]].
4. **Incentives** — extrinsic motivators (monetary + recognition) that supplement intrinsic drive.
5. **Context** — documented institutional knowledge that encodes good judgment.
6. **Systems** — automation of context; repeatable processes that reduce cognitive load.
7. **Governance** — active monitoring and review to detect drift from intent.

## Application

When any team underperforms, leaders diagnose by walking the seven elements: which one is missing or broken? Case studies at the retreat (UX/CSAT, card downtime, core banking outage, customer complaints) demonstrated multi-framework failures with multi-framework fixes.
