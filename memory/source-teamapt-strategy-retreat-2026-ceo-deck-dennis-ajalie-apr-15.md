Presented by [[Dennis Ajalie]] (CEO), [[Frank Atashili]] (CPO), [[Damilare Ogunnaike]] (Monnify Lead) at [[2026 Executive Strategy Retreat]], London, Apr 15, 2026. 32 slides.

## Summary

TeamApt CEO strategy deck covering scale metrics, financial performance trajectory (FY2022-2026), Monnify review and outlook, strategic pivot away from Moniepoint dependency, and execution plan.

## Key Points

### Scale & Current Position
- 6M average daily card transactions (Q1 2026)
- Q1 revenue ₦2.45B (125% of budget)
- 16 issuers on card switch
- ₦43B cash position
- 4 CBN licenses: Switching & Processing, Non-Bank Acquiring, PTSP, SuperAgent
- 98.11% average uptime
- 250+ staff

### Financial Performance Arc (FY2022–2026)
| Metric | FY2023 | FY2024 | FY2025 | FY2026B |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | ₦27.1B | ₦53.6B | ₦16.4B | ₦16.9B |
| Gross Margin | 95% | 96.4% | 81% | 76% |
| PAT | (₦3.6B) | ₦27.3B | (₦8.7B) | ₦0.07B |

- FY2024 peak driven by TSA fee from Moniepoint (non-recurring)
- FY2025 reset: TSA ceased, revenue dropped to ₦16.4B, opex not yet rationalized
- FY2026: first positive PBITDA year on product-driven revenue
- FY2023-24 PBITDA inflated by non-recurring FX items

### FY2026 Revenue Budget: ₦16.9B
| Line | Amount | Share |
|---|---|---|
| Monnify (Online) | ₦8.6B | 50.8% |
| ATS Cards (DCIR) | ₦3.3B | 19.6% |
| Consolidated Switch | ₦3.2B | 18.8% |
| TPP (New) | ₦1.1B | 6.4% |
| Consolidated DD | ₦0.7B | 4.4% |

- Monthly trajectory: ₦638M (Jan) → ₦2,449M (Dec), +45% jump in April from Switch and TPP ramp
- PBITDA breakeven in July; H1 loss ₦1.77B (investment phase); full year ₦0.23B positive
- Opex ₦16.2B (-32% vs FY2025): Employee 58%, Cloud 29%, Sales & Marketing 5%
- Cost base largely fixed — incremental revenue flows almost directly to PBITDA

### Card Pricing — Revenue Opportunity Forgone
- Current: 0.1% capped at ₦10; TeamApt share 30% = ₦3/txn
- Market rate: 0.3% capped at ₦15; average fee ₦7.50/txn
- At 5.5M daily transactions: current ₦5.94B/year vs market rate ₦14.85B/year
- **Gap: ₦8.91B/year unrealized revenue**
- Decision required: final fee percentage and cap

### 86% Moniepoint Revenue Dependency — Honest Diagnosis
- Most features, transaction volume, and revenue depend on Moniepoint's card payments ecosystem
- Imbalanced relationship: Moniepoint controls timelines, delays delivery, reports large revenue while constraining TeamApt from charging market rates
- TSA fees ceased — removed largest revenue line
- Strategic framing: "Build alongside, then diversify" — not a replace story

### Six Growth Levers for Diversification
1. **International Card Payments** — Visa & MC acquiring (TPP), newly obtained Visa license + MC processor cert; target $1M+ revenue
2. **Direct Debit & MADD** — high-margin recurring rail; Phase 1: NIBSS BVN account linkage by June; Phase 2: single auth interface; Phase 3: industry adoption
3. **[[GoSubscribe]]** — DD + niche payments on POS (DSTV/GOTV, utilities, IPOs, micro-pensions, micro-health insurance); agent-led via 25K+ agents
4. **Strategic Partnerships** — increase bank count per product; new PayFac onboarding (GlobalPay, LendSqr, HabariPay, Paystack, Cyberpay); switch-to-switch integrations
5. **TSC (Settlement & Clearing)** — third-party settlement and clearing; Dennis discussed thresholds with NIBSS; no revenue numbers yet
6. **TSA Collections** — emerging opportunity; early days; no projections

### Monnify 2025 Performance & 2026 Outlook
- **2025:** Revenue ₦8.29B (72% of ₦11.1B target), Volume 599M (+37.4% YoY), Value ₦27T (+42.4%), but transacting merchants dropped 20.3% to 9,542
- Lost >50% VTU volume (63.9% → 24.5% of volume Jan '25 to Mar '26)
- 4 Ponzi incidents in 2025
- **2026 OKRs:** Payin 99.99%, Payout 99.99%, P99 3s, Checkout 75%, CSAT 99%, zero Ponzi incidents, ₦7.61B revenue, 604M volume
- **Q1 2026:** Revenue ₦1.77B (130% of budget), Merchants 4,650 (130%), Value ₦7.86T (106%), Volume 115.6M (95%)
- Gross margin declining: 91.18% (2025) → 81.19% (2026); Contribution margin 79.6% → 65.07%
- Strategic value: ₦52.3B daily average deposit (Mar 2026); top merchants include Kora Payments (₦7.04B), NALA (₦3.59B), Fincra (₦2.86B), Sporty Internet (₦2.66B)
- VA commoditization acknowledged — every competitor offers VAs at aggressive prices
- Pivot: international payments, product expansion, lifecycle optimization, developer-focused marketing
- Needs: Product Growth Partner, Product Managers, Engineering Lead

### International Payments — Validated Targets
- 5 merchants, $615K revenue, $38.9M txn value, 135K volume, 15,000 POS terminals intl-enabled
- Q1: Visa 3DS completed, MPGS live, $20K+ first txns
- Q2: MVP launch, Visa integration, 15K POS intl-enabled
- Q3: scheme integrations, auto routing, dispute portal
- Q4: scale to $1M+

### Implementation Status
- 17 banks engaged, 11 POS ATS live, 6 products active, 20+ switch partners
- Key 2025 completions: Access DD, Wema Web ATS, Stanbic POS, Ecobank POS, Parallex Verve POS, Polaris DD, Opay Account Switch, CoralPay Card Switch, Polaris Card Switch, Habari POS Acquiring
- NSS live: Polaris, Union, Wema, Fidelity, Zenith (FCMB SLA in review)
- Key blockers: NSS settlement readiness (FETS, Blusalt, Alatpay), commercial/SLA negotiations, partner technical readiness
