## Summary

TeamApt MANCo meeting on February 24, 2026, chaired by Ibukun Atoyebi. Major topics: financial reconciliation (reduced from 16 to 13 outstanding accounts worth ₦17Bn net), CBN compliance headaches on facility accounts held in TeamApt's name (Zenith Bank facility expiring May), Outpost migration (async replication tool blockers may shift cutover), ₦428M monthly loss (breakeven projected when TPP and DD go live), operations restructuring with dedicated leads across all businesses, PCIDSS/IMS audit preparations, legal update including Ponzi lawsuit defense strategy (Credit B class action).

## Key Points

- Financial reconciliation: 13 outstanding accounts worth ₦17Bn net, down from 16; target full resolution by end Q1
- CBN compliance: facility accounts in TeamApt name must move to MFB; Zenith Bank facility (₦100Bn) expires May
- AFS submission due March 10 with proof of resolved issues
- Outpost migration: async replication blockers; cutover tentatively moved to next weekend; Latifa must provide security sign-off
- January financials: ₦827M income from core activities (Monnify ₦540M, ATS ₦287M); ₦428M loss for month; ₦5Bn VAT payment required liquidating investments
- Operations restructured: dedicated leads for domestic switch, DD, TPP, PTSP
- ZenDesk migration caused WhatsApp channel lockout (1,500 merchants affected)
- DD exposure: ₦110M of ₦159M recovered; ₦35M outstanding with court orders to Sterling, Kuda, Polaris
- PTSA January: 213M transactions, ₦5.253T value, 99.4% success rate, 90% approval rate
- NSS progress: Zenith 54%, Union Bank 42%, Fidelity signed consent letter
- Hiring paused for engineering, compliance, and product management roles
- Headcount: 252 (62% male, 38% female); 2.4% monthly turnover
- Legal: 101 document requests in January (spike from audit); 4 ongoing litigation matters; Credit B class action defense
- Inbound phone support team for TeamApt planned by end of next quarter
