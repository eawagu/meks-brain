## Summary
Moniepoint's engineering leadership convened on April 1, 2026 to deliberate on multiple Head of Engineering (VP+ and Director/Sr. Director) candidates following batch interviews. The session surfaced calibration gaps in how interviewers assess director-level system design, and resulted in alignment to remove Low-Level Design (LLD) from the round-one scorecard and focus instead on trade-offs, failure points, and high-level architectural thinking.

## Key Points
- Overall signal: Most candidates rejected for lacking strategic depth, overbloated resumes, or fundamental technical errors.
- Calibration decision: Director/Sr. Director round 1 should focus on HLD (trade-offs, reliability, observability, failure points), NOT LLD.
- Decision rule: If assessment is a "maybe," default to "no" to conserve everyone's time.
- Resume inflation is endemic; screener ([[Chris Purkis]]) will ask more specific questions about claimed accomplishments.
- Candidate verdicts: Nish — No (insufficient depth under pressure); Deepo — No (average, missed scaling/async); Himancho — No (unclear, changed problem statement); Shray — No (very basic design); Chaz — Yes (strong architecture, advance to next round); Loan — No (unnecessarily complex, poor judgment); Son — No (vague, fundamental errors: claimed Cassandra is ACID-compliant, Redis for 100% durability).
- Goldman Sachs VP titles are inflated (≈ Senior Software Engineer) — not a reliable leveling signal.
- Domain-specific payments experience not required for director-level; 60-70% of design concerns are transferable.
