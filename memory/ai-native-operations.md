## Definition

**AI-Native Operations** is the operational thread of [[Project Phoenix]]'s AI-nativeness goal — embedding AI into Moniepoint's day-to-day workflows, not just customer-facing features. Originates from [[Tosin Eniolorunda]]'s February 2026 mandate that all functional managers rebuild their workflows using AI (via [[Eywa]]).

Access to Claude is coordinated through [[Michael Afolabi]].

## Application Patterns (as of Apr 2026)

### Inside CI&P (MFB Cards)

AI automation priorities agreed on 2026-04-21:

1. **Chargeback & Issue Resolution** (highest priority) — [[Aima]] / [[Precious]]' team
   - Automating dispute intake
   - Automating routing
   - Automating status communication
2. **Logistics & Production** — [[Philip]]'s team
   - Automating card file generation
   - Automating inventory requests
   - Automating regional dispatch processes
3. **General workflow automation** — replacing manual Jira request logging with AI-driven ticketing
   - Tool evaluation led by [[Rumulo]] (likely [[Romulo Braga]]) with [[Tracy Ojaigho]] scheduled to review

### Strike Team Mandate

The CI&P strike team has two primary mandates: (1) build the new [[Card Management System]], (2) drive AI automation initiatives within card operations. These are co-equal priorities.

## Eywa as the Nervous System

[[Eywa]] is the AI layer connecting every function's context and AI skills. Artifact hierarchy: Platform → Team → Capability → Feature → User Story → Acceptance Criteria. Currently powered by Claude; model-agnostic by design.

## Pattern — AI as Operational Tier, Not Just Product Tier

MoniePoint's AI-native framing explicitly targets internal operations (dispute processing, logistics, ticketing) alongside customer-facing product capabilities. This distinguishes Phoenix's AI-nativeness from "AI feature" programmes elsewhere.
