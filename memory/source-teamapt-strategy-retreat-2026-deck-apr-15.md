## Summary

22-slide Strategy Retreat deck for [[TeamApt]] Day 2 (Apr 15, 2026). Agenda: 2026 goals/objectives, OKRs, 2025 highlights/lowlights, Q1 2026 achievements, Payment Gateway performance ([[Monnify]]), Direct Debit ([[GoSubscribe]]/[[VAS]]), TPP (international cards, issuer & acquirer, [[ACT]]), Switching and Processing ([[Juliana Account Switch]] and [[ATS]]), Business Development, ratification items.

## Key Points

- **2026 target:** break even at NGN 7.5bn revenue ("we are 4 years old"). Relies on Moniepoint sales machine for [[VAS]], ENS, DNS, Card Payments.
- **2026 strategic goals:** (1) increase DD banks on rails ([[Zenith Bank]], [[GTBank]], [[FCMB]], [[Union Bank]], [[Keystone]] in Q2); (2) complete Issuing leg of MC/Visa Processing Certification (MIP); (3) integrate 80% of switching banks onto TeamApt NSS; (4) launch Acquirer and Issuer Processing for ≥2 commercial banks on MC+Visa; (5) launch [[GoSubscribe]] in May (recurring subscriptions on POS).
- **Q1 2026 achievements:** FTSA live on [[Union Bank]] + [[Keystone]]; [[Visa]] acquiring licence obtained + endpoint implementation; 45,000+ Direct Debit transactions in single month beta; GTBank DD API access secured for Tier-1 expansion; Truecaller partnership (46M users) for GoSubscribe distribution; UPSL alternate PTSA route completed (slide 7).
- **Performance metrics cited:** 14.5M daily transactions; 98.11% average uptime; Switch 2025 avg success 98.75% (vs 2024 88.83% approval); Monnify 2025 avg success 99.57% (vs 2024 96.09%); Monnify 2025 collections 80.54% (vs 2024 38.36%).
- **Strategic partnerships:** integration with friendly switching/processing PSPs for FIs not directly connected; leverage [[MADD]]/[[MPGS]]; Go-Subscribe launch partners: Awabah, Moniepoint MFB, Habari POS.
- **Op efficiency:** strengthen collaboration with existing partner banks; address application deficiencies; competitive positioning via best-in-class + value for money; leverage Moniepoint Group brand while building TeamApt/Monnify brand identity.
- **Content gaps:** slides 3 (OKRs), 4, 8, 10–11, 17 appear to be chart/section title slides without transcribed text — the raw numeric data lives in embedded visuals not captured in export.
