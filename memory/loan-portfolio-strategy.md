## Strategic Goals (2026)

- **180,000 active loans** by year-end; ~1,000 active as of end-March.
- YTD loan volume 63B (5% behind 66.3B target due to January "disaster month").
- Sales productivity 1.8 loans/productive agent in March, target 2–2.5 by year-end.

## Four Business Objectives

1. Adoption (penetration).
2. Loan book growth (GLPV).
3. Risk-adjusted revenue.
4. Risk management.

## Portfolio Shape

Portfolio has crashed toward smallest loans due to volume-based sales incentive. Segments:
- **Large (200M+)**: revenue flat — retention-at-all-costs strategy.
- **Mid (5–15M)**: "absolutely in love with" segment — strong performance.
- **Mid (50–200M)**: too small — opportunity for growth.
- **Small tickets**: high volume but low value; even 100% penetration = ~50% of portfolio value.

Mid-segment is the biggest value-growth lever but currently shrinking, hypothesis = price too high. Price-drop campaign in progress to test elasticity.

## Product Pipeline

- **[[Digital Lending Product]]** — essential for reach given verification cost economics.
- **[[Asset Financing Strategy]]** — solar + smartphones for SMEs via partner model.
- **[[Big-Ticket Lending Strategy]]** — shift from cash disbursement to activity-financing (pay suppliers directly).
- Verification experiment (1 vs 2 guarantors up to 5M) shows no cohort-performance difference — justifies friction reduction.

## Risk Models

- [[Probability of Default Model]] (Yuki) launching next month.
- [[Affordability Model]] (Yuki) replacing simplistic model.

## Field Operations

Field credit officers now managing 400–500 loans each (up from 100). Hiring fails in remote areas. Exploring [[Paavo]]-model of hiring + training locals without prior experience.
