## Summary

Frank Atashili's PM & Business Leads briefing deck (updated March 2026 version, Internal — Confidential). Covers: operating/platform split with product experience layer (OpCos) consuming the processing & infrastructure layer (TeamApt-owned platforms); three-stage migration plan over six months; TSP capabilities and strike team composition; OKR commitment and handover plan; per-platform capability detail (Card Issuance, Card Acceptance, Payment Gateway, Identity); new Git + AI Specifications workflow; seven action items for the week.

## Key Points

- **Operating/Platform split:** Product experience layer owned by OpCos (Moniepoint MFB, MonieWorld, GlobalWire, future entities) — consumer/merchant-facing features, UX, product decisions, sales, ops, compliance. Processing & Infrastructure layer owned by Platform Companies ([[TeamApt]] — CBN-licensed switching & processing) — TSP, Card Issuance, Card Acceptance, CBA, Loom, Clearing & Settlement, Tokenisation. Multi-tenant, country-configurable, shared across all group entities.
- **Three-stage migration (6 months, strangler fig with feature flags, no big bang):**
  - **Stage 1 — Core Capabilities & Kenya Discovery (Months 1–2, end May 2026):** one global TSP ready for NG/UK/Kenya; PAY_OUT first end-to-end transaction type; 14 engineers across 2 strike teams; all 3rd-party integrations via adapters; common backend core (CBA, Cosmos); PM process artifacts finalised & tested; Kenya onboarding & payment discovery.
  - **Stage 2 — All Platforms + Alpha Launch (Months 3–5):** capability decomposition across all platforms; merge development (NG, UK, fit-for-Kenya); unified backend & frontend (mobile + web); **CHANGE FREEZE begins (~4 months)**; alpha launch to internal users; strangler fig parallel to existing platform; exception process headed by [[Tosin]].
  - **Stage 3 — Beta & Commercial Roll-Out (Month 6):** beta 50 business + 50 personal users; quantitative go/no-go gate (CPO + CTO) — error rate, payment success, p99 latency; phased commercial roll-out; deprecation of legacy platforms; pre-defined rollback criteria.
- **TSP capabilities:** Card Switching & Processing (PTSA connections, FEP routing, 3DS via ACS/DS/MPI, ~42–50% POS share); Account Switching & Processing (inter-bank routing, NIBSS NIP, Juliana Account Switch, account transfer rails); VAS Switching & Processing (44 provider integrations; airtime/data/bills); Direct Debit & Mandates (MADD processing, GoSubscribe billing, SAFE tokenisation, mandate lifecycle).
- **Domain vs TSP boundary:** Domain platforms decide WHAT to pay; TSP decides HOW (routing, fees, compliance screening, posting, rail dispatch). Callers never build postings.
- **TSP Strike Team:** Technical PM [[Oluwabunmi Oyefisayo]] ("Bunmi") formerly Switch & TPP Engineering product lead — now reports to [[Frank Atashili]] on TSP delivery, daily coord with [[Ravi Veluguleti]]. Confirmed: [[Muhammad Siddiqui (Yusuf)]] (TPP Switch Engineering) — core switch architecture / legacy bridging; [[Christopher Ogbosuka]] (Juliana Switch NDS) — clearing & settlement (TACHA) sustained in TSP; [[Abeeb Ahmad]] (Juliana Switch NDS) — payment flow migration; Sulaiman + UK Card Lead (UK, Alex's network) — UK rails integration (ClearBank, CoP) + card switching multi-market; 2-3 additional UK engineers for adapter layer. 2 strike teams split 14 implementation chunks for PAY_OUT. Weeks 1-2 ramp, 3-6 parallel build, 7-8 live validation.
- **Holding the Fort coverage:** [[Wycliffe Ochieng']] double-hats — covers [[Kevin Ng'Eno]]'s Switching Solutions + [[Abdulgafar Obeitor]]'s AptPay Suite; Gafar + [[Syed Ali (Yasir)]] support Wycliffe on AptPay; [[Taiwo Ogundipe]] + [[Olawale Adegboyega]] hold TPP Engineering jointly (Taiwo takes PM from Bunmi, Wale EM coverage); [[Abiodun Famoye]] + Yasir hold CDD continuity; [[Daniel Ojinaka]] continues as CDD Business Lead. Existing SRE teams unchanged ([[Saheed Yusuf]] NDS, [[Ugochukwu Ebirika]] AptPay).

- **OKR commitment:** "Q1 2026 OKRs remain sacrosanct." Phoenix is additive. Existing delivery commitments are non-negotiable. Any unmet Q1 OKRs continue in parallel. Escalate conflicts early.

- **Action items this week:** (1) document capabilities (all PMs); (2) identify code seam where team's validation ends and fund movement begins (TSP callers); (3) review TSP's SPI contracts — InboundRequest, TransferContext, CardContext, VasContext (TSP callers); (4) map team's TSP transaction types across 19 types and migration phases (TSP callers); (5) start AI tools training (everyone); (6) review Ravi's reverse-engineered spec for your domain (all PMs & EMs); (7) flag dependencies (all PMs).
- **Git + AI Specifications workflow:** (1) PM + AI tool develops structured change specification (country-agnostic, capability-focused); (2) AI generates epics/stories from spec, engineers review, version-controlled in Git; (3) engineering builds from spec (spec = source of truth; deviations visible via Git diff). Rationale: context lives in repos not heads; specs machine-readable for AI build/test/validate; version-controlled for troubleshoot/rollback.
- **Card Issuance capabilities:** Card Lifecycle (CI-CM-CL), EMV Data Prep (CI-CM-ED), Card Products (CI-CM-CP), PIN Management (CI-CM-PM), Card Controls (CI-CM-CC), Hot Card Mgmt (CI-CM-HC), Authorization Engine (issuer on-us). Boundary: TSP owns card switching (FEP routing, 3DS); Card Issuance owns CMS, card programs, BIN mgmt; auth decisions originate from Card Issuance, arrive via TSP.

- **Payment Gateway capabilities:** Collections (inbound, bank transfer/card/USSD/mobile money), Disbursements, VAS (product catalog — switching via TSP), Channels & Onboarding, Checkout Experience (hosted + embedded), Settlement & Reconciliation (execution moves to TSP).
