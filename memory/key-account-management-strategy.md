## Overview

Moniepoint's Key Account Management (KAM) strategy as framed at the 2026 Leadership Retreat (Day 1 and Day 2).

## Principle (Day 1)

- **Pareto alignment**: 10% of customers account for 55% of revenue.
- **White-glove service** on large clients — operational tier distinct from mass-market self-service.

## Purpose

- Contribution-margin lever for [[Profitability Targets 2026]].
- Defensible moat against competitors — retention-driven rather than CAC-driven.

## Expanded Structure (Day 2)

### Segmentation
Top % businesses segmented into three tiers: **Gold / Silver / Bronze**.

### Team Structure
- **Head of Key Account Management** leads the function.
- **Key Account Managers** report to the Head, with specialized domain knowledge for specific business sectors.
- **KAM sales operations team** provides dedicated issue resolution for high-net-worth businesses and conducts structured follow-ups.

### Goals
- Retention.
- Specialized services.
- Cross-sell.
- Up-sell.

### Partnership Sub-Team
- Drives acquisition and lead generation.
- Targets trade unions and associations.

### Cross-Cutting
- CAM team is **global** and **separate**, managing all merchants meeting a defined profile, regardless of whether they fall under the DNS or ENS teams.
- Has its own reporting structure.

## Multi-Layered Merchant Relationship Initiative (late 2023 → ongoing)

- Previously, relationships were heavily concentrated at BRM level — poor connections at higher merchant management.
- [[Babatunde]] and Tosi ([[Tosin Eniolorunda]]) now physically accompany team members to meet with merchants — stronger multi-layered relationships.
