## State (April 2026)

- **Procurement**: Q4'25 730K devices → Q1'26 curtailed to stop arbitrary buying. Memory price inflation expected.
- **Unit economics**: avg new terminal ~$45 cost; target $1–$2 reduction. ~₦2B unpaid logistics bills in process.
- **Write-offs up ~260%** vs Q4'25.
- **Revenue**: activation +6%, refunds -62%. Swaps erode economics (0.4% swap return vs ~38% activation). Maintenance ₦1,500/BO down 9% against ~$15/device cost.
- **Fleet**: ~5M ever procured, ~3.3M deployed. [[MP35 Fleet]] scale: ~2.2M MP35D, ~1.46M deployed. NBV breakdown in dispute (22B vs ~50B).
- **Idle**: 160K+ refurbished not redeployed; 200K+ new idle; ~664K total idle in DNS.
- **Faulty**: ~176K faulty in DNS; >140K at repair centers. 163K repaired MP35 still undeployed. Facilities (e.g., Magodo) overwhelmed.

## Structural Issues

- [[MP35P 4G Upgrade]] decision: discontinue MP35P or upgrade motherboards. Technical fix reduced failures 1.7% → 0.3%, but 3G perception penalty persists.
- [[Device Cannibalization Risk]]: past 200K loss from cannibalized/sold/disposed devices; controls pending.
- [[Competitor Demarketing]] by [[OPay]] — claims 3G/Android 5 illegal; orchestrates swaps.

## Proposed Allocation Fix

[[Capacity-Based Allocation Model]]: capacity-to-deploy as the deployment cap; BE as buffer/priority, not quantity driver. Monthly caps based on prior deployment; co-location for pickups. Anti-gaming (proxy account caps) required.

## Financial Options on MP35P

- Immediate write-off.
- Invest in 4G upgrade.
- Free-give to drive commission revenue (small merchants often refuse even free MP35P).
- Regulatory-contingent 4-year controlled write-off (preferred).
