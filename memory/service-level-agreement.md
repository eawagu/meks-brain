## Overview
A [[Service Level Agreement]] (SLA) is a formal agreement defining service-delivery expectations and obligations between parties — typically specifying availability, performance, support response times, and (under [[ISO 27001]] A5.20) security requirements.

## TeamApt Findings (Apr 2026)
Per the [[TeamApt Management Review 19_04_2026]]:

### ISMS A5.20 OFI
- Current state: SLAs in place with [[OPEX]] and [[Cybervergent]], but security requirements (e.g., audit rights) are not consistently included across all SLAs.
- Remediation: ensure the standardized security annex/requirements defined in the Vendor Policy are addressed in all SLAs where applicable.

### Service Management Risk (Moderate)
- Supplier/bank SLA breaches: failing to meet contractual obligations.
- Control: business leads confirm obligations at contract stage; legal team investigates suspected breaches identified during service monitoring.
