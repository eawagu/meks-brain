CTO Role Scope defines the boundary of technology accountability held by a Chief Technology Officer — which functions, teams, and decisions the role is directly responsible for versus delegated elsewhere. Varies significantly across organizations based on size, operating model, and whether platform/product/infrastructure are centralized or distributed.

## Common Scope Variants

- **Full-stack CTO** — all technology functions (product engineering, platform, SRE, security, operations)
- **Platform-centric CTO** — platform infrastructure only; product engineering reports to business units or CPO
- **Operations-only CTO** — running production systems; engineering execution elsewhere
- **Hybrid** — depends on company phase and strategic priorities
