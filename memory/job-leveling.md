## Definition

Job leveling assigns named levels to roles within an organization, each with a defined scope of work, autonomy, and expectations. A well-designed framework makes level boundaries legible — so that the difference between a Senior Associate and a Principal is observable in behavior, not just tenure.

## Why It Matters

Without explicit leveling, compensation, promotion, and hiring decisions are made on implicit criteria that differ manager-to-manager. Leveling frameworks force explicit calibration: "does this person's demonstrated scope match the level we're hiring for?"

## Key Dimensions (typical)

- **Autonomy**: how much direction does this person need to deliver results?
- **Scope**: individual task, team, multi-team, department, company?
- **Leadership**: mentoring, managing, or setting strategic direction?
- **Knowledge depth**: competent, expert, world-class/thought leader?
