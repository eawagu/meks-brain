## Overview

2026 targets for the Moniepoint card business, presented by [[Tosin Eniolorunda]] at the [[Moniepoint 2026 Leadership Retreat UK]] Day 1.

## Targets

- **23 million cards** sold.
- **1.3 billion** transactions processed.
- **#2 market position**.

## 2025 Baseline

- 7.97 trillion in transactions processed.
- 13.3M cards sold.
- Market share 5.9% → 11.7% (+97% YoY).
- Market position 7th → 3rd (behind OP and UBA).

## Card Unit Economics

- Production cost: ₦1,500 per card.
- Sold for: ₦1,000 — distribution subsidy.
- The ₦1,000 reward funds the sales distribution network.
- ₦2.2B of ₦13.3B card sales funded customer acquisition.
- Lack of complete LTV vs fully-loaded CAC view flagged.

## Product Roadmap

- **Tap-to-pay** (card + mobile) — pending Apple Pay (blocked on token service provider).
- **Virtual cards**.
- **Business cards**.
- Transsion partnership to increase NFC-enabled phones for tap-to-pay adoption.

## Q1 2026 Notes

- March card sales below budget — intentional: stopped selling to business customers to improve transaction quality.
- March Mastercard back-billing (Dec–Mar) inflated apparent monthly scheme costs.
- Transaction revenue outpacing scheme fees; web transactions yield higher revenue mix.

## CBN ATM Rule

- 1 ATM per 7,500 cards issued — significant deployment/operational cost (sourcing cash, custodians) flagged as a business consideration.
