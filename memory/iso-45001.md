## Overview
[[ISO 45001]] is the international standard for Occupational Health and Safety Management Systems (OHS).

## TeamApt Implementation
Per the [[TeamApt Management Review 19_04_2026]], ISO 45001 is one of five standards within the [[Integrated Management System]] at [[TeamApt]]. Documented objectives:
- Develop and maintain an emergency response framework targeting zero workplace casualties
- Implement proactive hazard identification via inspections, ergonomic assessments, employee reporting
- Establish preventive measures against accidents, injuries, occupational illnesses (office and remote)
- Ensure prompt incident response including first aid, medical referral, post-incident follow-up
- Maintain compliance with contractual, regulatory, and legal H&S obligations applicable to fintech
- Provide periodic training on health, safety, ergonomics, and emergency preparedness

## OHS Risk Register (Apr 2026) — All Major
| Risk Category | Description | Controls |
|---|---|---|
| Ergonomic | Poor home workstation setup | Ergonomic assessment + support tools |
| Health (Visual) | Prolonged screen exposure | Screen-break policy + awareness training |
| Psychological | Isolation/stress from remote work | Mental health and wellness programs |
| Electrical | Unsafe home electrical setups | Home electrical safety checklist |
| Fire Safety | Fire outbreak at shared facility | Pre-use safety verification + briefing |
| Physical Safety (Moderate) | Slips, trips, falls | Safety walkthrough pre-use |

## SWOT Context
- Strengths: regulatory compliance culture ([[CBN]], [[NDPC]], [[NITDA]], AML/CFT), skilled workforce, existing H&S policies, senior management commitment, centralised admin ownership of OHS
- Weaknesses: low perceived OHS risk in office/remote settings, limited OHS competency, unstructured incident reporting
- Opportunities: alignment with Nigerian Factories Act and ILO conventions, brand trust, integration with [[ISO 27001]] / [[ISO 22301]]
- Threats: enforcement gaps, resistance to change, cost sensitivity, physical security risks in field locations, overlooked psychosocial/ergonomic risks
