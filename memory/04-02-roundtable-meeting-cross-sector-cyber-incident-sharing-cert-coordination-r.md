## Summary

A cross-sector roundtable convened Nigerian banks, regulators, and national cybersecurity bodies to address fragmented incident response. Participants agreed to activate a unified sharing ecosystem anchored by [[Nigeria National CERT]], coordinated by [[NGCAC]]/[[NGSAT]], and aligned with [[CBN]] supervisory requirements. The Cybercrime Act 2015 mandates 7-day reporting for critical information infrastructure, but all parties agreed this is operationally too slow — banks should target near-real-time alerts within CBN's stricter 2–3 hour window.

## Key Points

- Banks and CISOs must share anonymized incident and vulnerability data rapidly to prevent repeat attacks; the former "bank fraud forum" model should be reinstated under NGCAC coordination and extended across sectors.
- Legal and supervisory frameworks require faster-than-mandated reporting; government designation via the [[National Security Advisor]] applies; non-reporting is sanctionable while reporting itself is not punitive.
- Cultural distrust and fear of reputational damage are primary barriers to sharing; anonymized, actionable formats and regular roundtables are the proposed response.
- [[NCC CERT]] and [[NFI CERT]] are to collaborate under a consolidated national incident response plan to be shared within 48 hours.
- Fraud prevention requires strengthened KYC, addressing insider threats, and cross-ecosystem collaboration with telecoms and law enforcement on SIM-based scams, mule accounts, and cross-channel fraud.
- Legal freeze constraints (72-hour release without court order) hinder fund recovery; reform or interim MOUs are required.
- Vulnerability management: pre-go-live security assessments mandated for cloud deployments; continuous testing, developer training (including AI-assisted coding risks), and third-party risk frameworks required.
- The forthcoming banking–exchange "handshake" for digital assets will compress response windows; real-time monitoring, interdiction, and tracing capabilities are needed.
- [[National Orientation Agency]] to partner on multi-language public awareness campaigns reinforcing KYC.

## Priority Actions

- [[NGCAC]]/[[NGSAT]] to onboard all bank CISOs to the national sharing platform and schedule recurring coordination meetings.
- Clarify critical infrastructure designations; enforce CBN's 2–3 hour reporting requirement with escalation and sanctions.
- Reinstate anonymized bank fraud forum; publish identity-protecting sharing guidelines.
- Share draft National Incident Response Plan; activate sectoral CERT coordination.
- Enforce pre-go-live security reviews; finalize third-party risk management frameworks.
- Establish joint protocols with telecoms for SIM-related fraud; reform freeze timelines.
- Continuous insider vetting and access governance across banking and telecom ecosystems.

## Unresolved Items

- Executors, onboarding steps, and timelines for national platform not yet assigned.
- Formal governance and trust framework for anonymized sharing pending.
- Enforcement specifics for pre-go-live assessments unclear.
- Detailed reporting formats, secure channels, and SLAs undefined.
- A referenced program will be stopped due to infeasibility — scope, dependencies, and stakeholder mitigation unclear.
