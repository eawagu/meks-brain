## Summary

Full [[TeamApt]] MD/CEO KPI Scorecard for FY 2026 — a confidential Board-facing performance rubric. Covers 7 categories (Financial 25%, Strategic Growth 20%, Technology 15%, Enterprise Clients 15%, Governance/Risk/Compliance 10%, Stakeholder/Brand 8%, People 7%). Uses a 6-point scale (1.0 = very materially below expectations → 6.0 = extraordinary and exceptionally above).

## Key Points

- **Category weights:** Financial Performance & Profitability 25%; Strategic Growth & Market Position 20%; Technology Excellence & Operational Performance 15%; Enterprise Client Growth & Retention 15%; Corporate Governance, Risk & Compliance 10%; Stakeholder Management & Brand 8%; People, Culture & Organisational Health 7%.
- **Financial targets at "Meets Expectations" (3.0):** Revenue growth 35–45% YoY; EBITDA margin 20–28%; cost-to-income ratio 55–65%; ROIC 15–20%.
- **Strategic targets:** ≥80% strategic plan milestones on time/in budget; 2 new African markets; ≥3 new product launches per year.
- **Technology targets (15% total, 3 sub-KPIs):** Platform uptime ≥99.9% (6% weight); SLA compliance ≥90% across onboarding/txn processing/support (5% weight); zero critical cybersecurity incidents with ISO 27001 maintained (4% weight).
- **Enterprise client targets:** Hit target new-client acquisition; ≥90% retention with zero top-20 churn; grow ARPC per plan with ≥2 products per enterprise client.
- **Governance targets:** Zero material regulatory breaches across Group; 100% of Board packs ≥5 business days before meetings; ≥90% internal audit findings remediated on time with Group Risk Framework implemented.
- **Stakeholder targets:** Investor satisfaction ≥75%; TeamApt top 5 African fintech infrastructure brand; CEO ≥4 thought leadership engagements/year; ≥3 strategic partnerships.
- **People targets:** Employee engagement ≥75%; critical-talent voluntary attrition ≤10%; ≥70% succession coverage with at least one successor for critical Group leadership roles.
- **Scale definitions:** Each KPI has distinct 1.0–6.0 descriptors. 1.0 typically implies Board escalation or intervention; 6.0 implies external industry recognition or benchmarking.
- **Regulators referenced:** [[CBN]] (referenced in governance and product innovation); external auditors (governance section).
