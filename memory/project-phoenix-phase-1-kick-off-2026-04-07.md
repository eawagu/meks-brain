## Summary

Official Project Phoenix Phase 1 kick-off meeting on April 7, 2026, led by Frank Atashili. The meeting launched the strategic initiative to build the Transaction Switching and Processing (TSP) platform as the organization's core system ("circulatory system"), mandated adoption of AI-driven workflows (Claude AI for spec-to-Jira pipeline), established a dedicated strike team, and restructured engineering assignments across teams.

## Key Points

- TSP designated as core priority — unified platform consumable by multiple countries and entities (banks, switches), with account switching and card switching as initial capabilities
- AI workflow mandate: all PMs and engineers must immediately adopt Claude AI-driven workflows using detailed "change specs" to generate Jira tasks, epics, and stories
- Strike team: Bunmi Oyefisayo as PM, Ysef and Chris as key engineering for clearing and settlement
- Engineering restructuring: Ketan Dhamasana → card issuance engineering; Wycliffe Ochieng' → EM for switch and app-based suite; Gaffa and Yasir maintain business continuity
- PM transitions: Taiwo Ogundipe → PM for TPP engineering; Kevin Ngeno assumes Bunmi's OKR/PM responsibilities; handovers must complete within the week
- Direct debit strategy: prioritized for revenue before phase 2 migration; "go subscribe" launch determines future architectural placement
- Existing business continuity: Q1 OKRs and commitments remain unchanged despite platformization
- Phase 2 defers card issuance, card acceptance, and other features
- Organizational model: transition from legacy TeamApt to platform-based architecture with Operating Companies (Opcos) and IP Companies (IPCos)
- Quality assurance: human oversight mandatory to mitigate AI hallucinations; APMs serve as QA role for requirements
